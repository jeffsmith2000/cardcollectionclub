One Piece TCG Starter Deck Guide 2026: Which Deck Should You Buy?

March 31, 2026 · 11 min read · Card Collection Club AU

The One Piece Card Game just got its biggest shakeup yet. April 1, 2026 marks the first Standard rotation — Block 1 cards (OP-01 to OP-04, Starter Decks 1-10) are rotating out of Standard play. If you're picking up the game now, this guide will steer you toward the right starter deck so you're not buying into cards that are about to become illegal in Standard.

Rotation Warning — April 2026

Block 1 cards (OP-01 through OP-04 and Starter Decks ST01-ST10) rotate out of Standard on April 1, 2026. Do NOT buy ST01-ST10 starter decks for competitive Standard play. They're fine for casual/collecting only.

How Deck Building Works

Quick primer if you're brand new:

  • Leader Card (1) — Defines your playstyle and which colour cards you can use
  • Main Deck (50 cards) — Characters, Events, and Stage cards. Max 4 copies of any card. Must match your Leader's colour identity.
  • DON!! Deck (10 cards) — Your energy source. Separate from main deck.

Best Starter Decks for 2026 (Post-Rotation)

Top Pick: ST28 Yamato — Yellow Balanced Midrange

Why Yamato is #1 for Beginners

Safe, reliable, easy to pick up

Teaches resource management naturally

Strong in midrange fights with late-game tools

Yellow's trigger mechanic is satisfying and intuitive

If you're buying one starter deck and you want the smoothest learning experience, it's Yamato. The deck teaches you how the game flows without overwhelming you with complex combos. It plays well against other starter decks out of the box, and Yellow's defensive tools mean you'll survive long enough to learn from your mistakes.

Best Aggro: ST23 Shanks — Red Tempo

Simple, fun, and satisfying. Shanks teaches you the basics of attacking and combat math. Red decks are the most straightforward — play characters, swing face. If you want to feel powerful early, this is your deck.

Limitation: Out of the box, it'll struggle against upgraded decks at locals. Budget ~$30-50 AUD in singles to make it competitive.

Best Budget Upgrade: ST25 Buggy — Blue

The best value proposition in the current starter lineup. Buggy is dirt cheap to upgrade into a genuine locals-viable deck. Blue's bounce mechanic is fun, and the deck rewards you for understanding tempo and timing.

Budget upgrade path: Buy 2x ST25, combine the best cards, add ~$20-30 in blue singles from OP13-OP15. You'll have a competitive locals deck for under $60 total.

Most Rewarding: ST26 Purple Luffy — High-Power Ramp

Purple Luffy ramps DON!! faster than any other colour, letting you slam down massive threats while your opponent is still setting up. It's the most skill-intensive starter deck — you need to plan 2-3 turns ahead — but when it clicks, you'll feel like you're cheating.

Warning: Higher skill floor. If this is your literal first card game, maybe start with Yamato or Shanks, then graduate to Purple.

Best Control: ST22 Ace & Newgate — Whitebeard Pirates

If you like grinding games out, outresourcing your opponent, and winning on card advantage rather than raw aggression, Ace & Newgate is your deck. Excellent card filtering and draw mechanics teach you the fundamentals of card economy.

Upcoming: ST30 Luffy & Ace (April 11, 2026)

This pre-built deck drops just after rotation and is designed for the new Standard format. If you can wait two weeks, this is likely the best out-of-box experience for 2026.

Coming July: ST31-ST36 — Fresh Start

Six new starter decks (one per colour) release July 11, 2026. If you're in no rush, these will be the definitive beginner entry point for the rest of the year. Built entirely for the post-rotation meta.

Where to Buy Starter Decks in Australia

  • Local card shops — Usually stock all current starters. Check our directory for shops near you.
  • EB Games / Zing — Most locations carry One Piece TCG starter decks
  • Online AU retailers — Leo Games, Mint Collectables, Odyssey TCG, TCGroup AU
  • Amazon AU — Sometimes discounted during sales events

Pro tip: Buy your starter deck at a local card shop and ask when they run One Piece weekly tournaments. Playing in person is the fastest way to learn, and most communities are incredibly welcoming to beginners.

Learning Together: LD-01 Learn Together Deck Set

If you're starting with a friend or partner, the Learn Together set (LD-01) contains three pre-built decks specifically designed for teaching the game. It's the best shared learning experience and great value for the price.

What NOT to Buy

  • ST01-ST10 (Block 1 starters) — Rotating out April 1. Fine for casual play, bad for competitive.
  • ST27 Marshall D. Teach — Powerful but brutally difficult for beginners. "Crazy learning curve" and requires deep meta knowledge to pilot effectively.
  • Any starter deck if your goal is purely collecting — Booster boxes have the chase cards. Starter decks are for playing.
