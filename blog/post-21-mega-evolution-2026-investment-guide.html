Pokemon Mega Evolution 2026 — The Complete Investment Guide

March 25, 2026 · 8 min read · Card Collection Club AU

The Mega Evolution era is back — and if you missed the original XY sets in 2013–2016, this is your second shot. Pokemon has launched a brand-new Mega Evolution block for the Scarlet & Violet era, and the cards coming out of these sets are already generating serious collector interest. Here's what you need to know before opening your wallet.

🔥 Bottom line up front: Storm Emerald (Mega Rayquaza ex, July 2026) looks like the set to hold. Rayquaza has driven some of the biggest price spikes in Pokemon history. Buy ETBs at RRP and sit on them.

The 2026 Mega Evolution Lineup

Pokemon is releasing five main sets in the Mega Evolution block throughout 2026. Each set focuses on different Mega Evolutions from the Legends: Z-A game. Here's the full release calendar:

Ascended Heroes (MEG1)

Released Jan 30, 2026. 290+ cards. The base set — Mega Charizard, Mega Blastoise, Mega Venusaur getting the full ex treatment.

Perfect Order (MEG2)

Released Mar 27, 2026. Mega Zygarde ex, Mega Starmie ex, Mega Clefable ex. Legends: Z-A focus.

Chaos Rising (MEG3)

Releasing May 22, 2026. Mega Greninja ex, Mega Pyroar ex, Mega Dragalge ex. Water and fire heavy set.

Storm Emerald (MEG5)

Releasing Jul 31, 2026. Mega Rayquaza ex. The one everyone's watching. Sky High rings a bell?

Why This Block Matters for Investors

The original Mega Evolution cards from XY are now worth serious money. Mega Charizard EX 69/108 in PSA 10 regularly sells for $800–1,500 AUD. The reason? Nostalgia, scarcity, and Charizard. This new block is attempting to recreate that energy with modern card quality and better artwork.

Key investment angles to watch:

  • Secret Rare Mega cards: Every set will have Special Illustration Rares (SIR) of key Mega Pokemon. These are the targets. Pull them, protect them, grade them.
  • ETB sealed speculation: Perfect Order ETBs at RRP ($79.95) look attractive if Zygarde becomes a tournament staple.
  • Storm Emerald boxes: Rayquaza cards have an almost supernatural hold on collectors. The 2015 Roaring Skies Rayquaza EX is still climbing. History suggests Storm Emerald boxes will not stay at RRP for long.

The Storm Emerald Opportunity

Rayquaza is arguably the most popular Pokemon for collectors who aren't just Charizard fans. The Roaring Skies (2015) Rayquaza EX FA is worth 10-15x its original purchase price today. The M Rayquaza EX Colorless card is PSA 10 gold territory.

Storm Emerald drops July 31, 2026. That's 4 months away. Here's what smart moves look like:

  • Pre-order 1–2 booster boxes at RRP ($199–215 AUD) from Australian retailers now
  • Buy ETBs at RRP ($79.95) — ETBs are the best sealed product for price-to-appreciation in Pokemon history
  • If Mega Rayquaza ex SIR pulls, grade them immediately (PSA or ACE grading)
  • Hold sealed products for 12–24 months minimum

✅ Jeff's Verdict: Buy 1–2 Storm Emerald ETBs at RRP and hold sealed. If you find booster boxes at retail, grab one. Don't overpay in the grey market — there's still 4 months before release. For Ascended Heroes and Perfect Order, open and chase the SIRs, then grade your best pulls.

What to Avoid

Not every Mega Evolution card is a winner. Avoid:

  • Bulk buying Chaos Rising — Greninja is popular but not at Rayquaza level for sealed speculation
  • Abyss Eye at premium prices — Darkrai is cool but hasn't historically driven box speculation
  • Grading common rares — Grading costs eat your margin on anything that won't grade PSA 9+
