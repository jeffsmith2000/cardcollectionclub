OP10 Royal Blood — First Impressions

OP10 dropped in Japan on January 25th and the secondary market has been absolute chaos. This set introduces the Celestial Dragon and Holy Knight archetypes, which are shaking up the competitive meta in a big way. But it's the collector cards that are stealing the show.

Shanks Manga Art SR — $260 AUD (JP)

This is THE card of the set. The artwork recreates the iconic Marineford panel where Shanks arrives to end the war, and Bandai absolutely nailed it. The manga-style treatment with selective colour highlights is stunning in person — I've seen photos but a mate in Tokyo sent me a video of the card under light and it's next level.

At $260 AUD for the Japanese version, it's already the most expensive card from a current set. English pre-orders are floating around $300-350 on speculation, but I'd wait — the EN release isn't until late March and pre-order premiums always get corrected.

Boa Hancock Alt Art — $180 AUD (JP)

The "waifu tax" is alive and well. This Hancock alt art is gorgeous — a full illustration of her using her powers with a detailed background. At $180 for the JP version, it's the second most expensive card in the set. The Hancock cards always hold a premium in One Piece TCG, and this one will likely climb when the EN version drops.

OP09 Cards Still Climbing

OP09 has been out for a few months now and several cards are still trending upward as the competitive meta settles and collectors catch up with what's actually rare.

Roronoa Zoro Alt Art Leader — $185 AUD

Up 22% this month. The Zoro alt art leader from OP09 is becoming the "must-have" card of the set. The artwork is the King of Hell three-sword style moment from Wano — arguably the most badass Zoro moment in the entire manga. Competitively viable AND collector-desirable is the dream combo for price appreciation.

I pulled one from a booster box (my only good pull from 3 boxes, mind you) and I'm holding. This card has $250+ written all over it within 6 months.

Eustass Kid Manga Art SR — $120 AUD

The sleeper hit of OP09. Kid manga art SRs were undervalued at $85-90 a month ago and have jumped 30% as people realise the pull rate on these is brutal. The manga art treatment is becoming the most collectible rarity tier in One Piece TCG — they're the "alt arts" of this game.

Yamato SP — $98 AUD

Yamato cards just sell. The character has a massive fanbase and every SP/alt treatment of Yamato gets scooped up immediately. At $98 the EN version is running about 60% above the JP price ($60 AUD), which is typical for popular character SPs in this game.

If you're primarily a collector and don't care about language, Japanese cards represent significantly better value. The card quality is also generally superior — thicker stock, better printing consistency.

My strategy: I buy JP for my personal collection and EN for anything I might sell later. The EN liquidity in the Australian market is much better — more buyers, faster sales, less friction.

Meta Shifts Driving Prices

The competitive side of One Piece TCG directly impacts card prices. Here's what's happening in the meta:

Winners

  • Trafalgar Law (OP01 Leader) — Up 25% to $75 AUD. Law decks are making a resurgence in the OP10 meta and the original OP01 leader is getting bought up as a budget-competitive option. This is a card I recommended buying at $40 back in September — glad to see it paying off.
  • Red/Purple aggro pieces — OP10 introduced cards that supercharge Red/Purple strategies. Older staples from OP05-OP07 that support these builds are getting pulled up.
  • Shanks-related cards — The new Shanks leader in OP10 has created demand for Shanks support cards from earlier sets. Cross-set synergy always drives price movement.

Losers

  • Black control pieces — Rob Lucci and Black-heavy strategies are struggling in the OP10 meta. Cards like Rob Lucci Alt Art Leader (OP05) have dropped 15% as competitive demand evaporates.
  • OP03/OP04 competitive staples — Cards that were good purely for gameplay with minimal collector appeal continue to decline as they become less relevant competitively.

  • OP09 Box (EN): $135 AUD — good value given the chase cards in this set
  • OP10 Box (JP): $95 AUD — the smart play is buying JP boxes now and holding singles for EN release hype
  • OP05 Box (EN): $185 AUD — climbing purely on Gear 5 Luffy demand
  • ST-01 Starter Deck (EN): $85 AUD sealed — tripled from RRP, driven by new player demand

My February Picks

  • Buy: Eustass Kid Manga Art SR (OP09) — still undervalued at $120 relative to other manga arts
  • Buy: Trafalgar Law OP01 Leader — meta resurgence + nostalgia = continued upside
  • Buy: JP OP10 booster boxes for hold — the EN release will spike JP single demand
  • Hold: Zoro Alt Art Leader — $250 target within 6 months
  • Sell: Nami SP if you're sitting on profits — the decline is gradual but real
  • Wait: Shanks Manga Art SR — let the EN release hype settle before buying

The One Piece TCG is the real deal. The competitive scene in Australia is growing every month, local shops are stocking more product, and the card quality keeps improving. If you're not in this game yet, February is a great time to start. 🏴‍☠️
