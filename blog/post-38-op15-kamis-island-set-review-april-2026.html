OP15 Adventure on Kami's Island: Full Set Review & Investment Guide

April 2, 2026 · 8 min read · Card Collection Club

One Piece OP15 has officially dropped in English as of April 3, 2026, and it's already making waves. The Skypiea-themed set features some of the most valuable chase cards we've seen in the One Piece TCG — including an unprecedented TWO Manga Rares. Here's everything you need to know about opening, investing, and collecting OP15.

What Makes OP15 Special?

This is the first One Piece set to include two Manga Rare cards in a single release. Typically sets feature just one of these ultra-premium chase cards, but OP15 delivers both an Enel and a Koby (from EB-04) Manga Rare. That alone makes this set historically significant.

The Skypiea arc is a fan favourite, and Bandai has done it justice with stunning artwork across the set. Leaders include Enel, Monkey D. Luffy, Bonney, Lucy (Sabo), and Brook — all with sought-after alternate art versions.

Top Chase Cards & Prices

🏆 Enel Manga Rare (OP15-118 SEC-SP) — $890–$1,482 USD

The crown jewel. Japanese market prices have ranged from ¥138,000 to over ¥160,000. This is the card everyone is hunting. The Comic Parallel / Super Parallel version is the most expensive One Piece card from any 2026 set so far.

💎 Boa Hancock SP (OP12-014 reprint) — ~$550 USD

Special Rare reprint of the beloved Boa Hancock. One Piece Waifu tax in full effect — this card commands serious money.

⚡ Monkey D. Luffy SEC (OP15-119) — ~$102 USD (Parallel Foil)

The other Secret Rare in the set. Luffy cards always have long-term demand. The parallel foil version is the one to chase.

🌩️ Monkey D. Luffy SP (ST26-005) — ~$400 USD

Special Rare Luffy from a starter deck reprint. High demand from both collectors and players.

⭐ Enel Leader Alt-Art (OP15-058) — ~$67 USD

Beautiful alternate art leader card. More accessible entry point for Enel collectors.

Is OP15 Worth Opening?

Short answer: yes, if you can get boxes near RRP.

The expected value per box is high thanks to the two Manga Rares dramatically inflating chase card values. Even standard Secret Rares and the alt-art leaders hold solid value. If you're a collector who enjoys opening packs, OP15 is one of the best sets to rip in 2026.

Tip: For pure investment, sealed boxes will likely appreciate over 12-18 months as supply dries up. The Skypiea arc has enormous nostalgia value, and the Enel Manga Rare creates sustained demand for sealed product.

Investment Outlook

  • Sealed boxes: Strong hold. Two Manga Rares = sustained demand for sealed product. Buy at or near RRP and sit on them.
  • Enel Manga Rare: If you pull one, hold it. These are the type of card that appreciates over years, not weeks.
  • Alt-art leaders: Good mid-range investments. Enel and Luffy alt-arts should appreciate as the set goes out of print.
  • Standard SECs: These will likely drop as more product gets opened. Wait to buy singles unless you need them for play.

Where to Buy in Australia

Most major AU card shops should have OP15 stock from April 3. Check with your local game store — many are doing release events and tournaments. Online retailers like TCGroup AU, Card Cave, and Gameology typically have competitive pricing on booster boxes.

⚠️ Watch Out For

Scalper pricing on launch day. OP15 is highly anticipated, so some sellers will inflate prices. Wait a week or two for restocks if you're not finding reasonable prices. Don't pay more than 20% above RRP for booster boxes.

Final Verdict

OP15 is a landmark set for the One Piece TCG. Two Manga Rares, iconic Skypiea artwork, and strong playable cards make it a must-have for both collectors and players. Whether you're ripping packs or stashing sealed boxes, this set delivers.

Rating: 9/10 — One of the best One Piece sets released in the English market to date.
