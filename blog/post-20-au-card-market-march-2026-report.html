Headline: Psyduck Munch on a Tear

The 2018 Japanese promo Psyduck Munch has been one of the biggest movers in Q1 2026. PSA 9 copies were selling for $900-1,500 USD in late 2025 and have surged to $1,700-2,500 USD ($2,500-3,600 AUD) in March 2026. Raw near-mint copies are up 33% in the last 30 days alone, hitting $1,069 USD.

The Munch promo series remains one of the most collectible Japanese promos ever produced. If you own one, hold it.

One Piece Market Thesis

The One Piece Card Game has transitioned from "niche anime TCG" to genuine collectible asset class. Key drivers:

  • Player base explosion since 2022 launch - Oceania Regionals drawing 1,000+ players
  • Sealed appreciation across almost every set (OP01-OP10 all above RRP)
  • Cultural tailwind from One Piece anime/manga continued popularity
  • Limited first prints creating genuine scarcity on early sets

The OP01 Wave 1 box at $8,000+ AUD is the blue-chip of this market. Full cases have sold for $115,000 AUD.

What to Watch in April 2026

  • OP15 English release (April 3) - Watch opening weekend prices on SECs. Buy singles after 2 weeks.
  • Perfect Order singles market - Mega Zygarde SAR will be the one to track.
  • Hobby Hangout XL Melbourne (April 26) - Major event, expect market activity around it.
  • Armageddon Expo Wellington (April 3-5) - NZ collectors, good buying opportunity.

Collector Tips for March/April

  • Buy sealed at RRP. Both Pokemon and One Piece sealed products have been appreciating. If you can get boxes at retail, do it.
  • Singles patience. Never buy singles in release week unless it is a must-have. Prices always dip 2-4 weeks post-release.
  • Convention season is here. CollectFest Melbourne (March 28-29) and multiple April events mean opportunities to buy, sell, and trade in person.
  • Grade your gems. If you are sitting on ungraded high-value cards, PSA turnaround times have improved. A PSA 10 can 3-5x a raw card's value.
