Best Card Shops in Melbourne 2026

J
John Ceron
February 12, 2026 · 11 min read

Melbourne is arguably the best city in Australia for card collecting. Between the dedicated card shops, gaming stores with solid TCG sections, vending machines popping up in shopping centres, and a thriving convention scene, you're never far from your next pull. I've been hitting Melbourne card shops for years and this is my honest rundown of the best spots in 2026.

For the full searchable list with maps and opening hours, check our Card Shop Directory.

🏆 Cherry Collectables

📍 Springvale & Online | ★★★★★

Cherry is the big dog of Australian card retail. Their Springvale warehouse store is a destination in itself — rows of sealed product, massive singles display cases, grading submission services, and competitive pricing. They do huge volumes which means they can undercut most competitors on sealed product.

Best for: Sealed product (best prices in Melbourne), bulk buying, grading submissions

Pro tip: Their website often has flash sales that beat in-store prices. Check both. New product drops (especially Pokemon) sell out fast in-store — line up early on release day or pre-order online.

Singles: Limited in-store but their online singles selection is growing. Don't expect to walk in and find specific chase cards — it's primarily a sealed product operation.

🎮 Good Games — Multiple Locations

📍 Melbourne CBD, Ballarat, Werribee, Epping, Ringwood + more | ★★★★☆

Good Games is the backbone of Australia's organised play scene. If you want to actually play TCGs — weekly tournaments, league nights, pre-release events — Good Games is where it happens. Their Melbourne CBD store on Lonsdale St is the flagship, with regular Pokemon, One Piece, and MTG events.

Best for: Organised play, tournaments, meeting other players, pre-release events

Pro tip: Sign up for their loyalty program. Points accumulate across all locations and the discounts stack up if you're a regular. Thursday nights are usually Pokemon league at CBD.

Singles: Varies wildly by location. CBD usually has a decent singles binder. Suburban stores are hit or miss. Don't expect competitive pricing on singles — their margin is higher than dedicated singles sellers.

🎲 Games Laboratory

📍 Melbourne CBD (Little Lonsdale St) | ★★★★☆

Games Lab is a Melbourne institution. It's been around for years and has a loyal following. The store is compact but well-stocked, with a good mix of TCGs, board games, and tabletop stuff. Their TCG section has grown significantly since the Pokemon boom and they now carry solid One Piece stock too.

Best for: Casual browsing, discovering new games, CBD convenience

Pro tip: They often have older sealed product that other stores have sold out of. If you're hunting for specific expansion packs or theme decks, Games Lab is worth checking.

Singles: Minimal. This is a sealed product and gaming accessories store. For singles, look elsewhere.

📦 Gameology

📍 Richmond (online warehouse + showroom) | ★★★★☆

Gameology started as an online retailer and their Richmond warehouse/showroom reflects that. It's more of a "come in, grab what you need, and go" experience than a hang-out spot. But their prices are consistently competitive on sealed product and they get allocation on hard-to-find sets.

Best for: Competitive sealed pricing, picking up online orders, hard-to-find products

Pro tip: Subscribe to their newsletter for restock notifications. They were one of the few places in Melbourne that had Prismatic Evolutions ETBs at RRP.

Singles: None. Sealed only.

🤖 Card Bot Vending Machines

📍 Highpoint, Chadstone, Westfield Doncaster, + more | ★★★☆☆

The Card Bot machines have popped up in shopping centres across Melbourne and they're honestly pretty great for impulse buys. They stock Pokemon, One Piece, Dragon Ball, and Yu-Gi-Oh booster packs and sometimes have ETBs and collection boxes. Prices are at or slightly above RRP.

Best for: Impulse buys, convenience, grabbing packs when you're at the shops with family

Pro tip: The Highpoint and Chadstone machines get restocked most frequently. Check if they have stock before making a special trip — they can be empty for days after a new set drops.

Singles: N/A — sealed product only through the machine.

💎 TCA Gaming (The Card Affair)

📍 Preston | ★★★★★

If you're after singles, TCA is the spot. They have an incredible singles selection for both Pokemon and One Piece, with competitive pricing and knowledgeable staff who actually collect. The Preston store has a real community feel — regulars hanging out, trades happening at the counter, genuine collector energy.

Best for: Singles (best selection in Melbourne), One Piece TCG, community vibes

Pro tip: They post new singles arrivals on Instagram. Follow them for first dibs on chase cards. Their One Piece singles selection is particularly strong — probably the best in Victoria.

🃏 Guf (Games @ Urf) — Multiple Locations

📍 Ballarat, Melbourne CBD, Geelong | ★★★★☆

Guf has been a regional powerhouse for years and their Melbourne CBD location has brought that energy to the city. Solid TCG events, reasonable pricing, and friendly staff. Their Ballarat store is a must-visit if you're out that way — it's one of the best regional card shops in Australia.

Best for: Regional collectors, events outside the CBD, Magic the Gathering (strongest MTG community)

Best Shops by Category

  • Best prices on sealed product: Cherry Collectables
  • Best singles selection: TCA Gaming
  • Best for tournaments/events: Good Games CBD
  • Best for One Piece TCG: TCA Gaming
  • Best CBD convenience: Games Laboratory
  • Best for online ordering: Cherry Collectables / Gameology
  • Best regional: Guf Ballarat

Melbourne Card Show Calendar

Melbourne gets regular card shows throughout the year. The main ones to watch for in 2026:

  • Card Fest Melbourne — Usually at Melbourne Showgrounds. 2-3 times per year. The biggest show in Victoria.
  • TCG World Melbourne — Growing event series at various venues. Check their socials for dates.
  • Local community shows — Pop up regularly in the eastern and southeastern suburbs. Facebook groups like "Melbourne Pokemon Card Collectors" and "Melbourne TCG Buy Sell Trade" post these frequently.

Tips for Melbourne Collectors

  • Join the Facebook groups. "Melbourne Pokemon Card Collectors" and "Melbourne TCG Community" are the most active. Deals, meetups, and trades happen daily.
  • Try the op shops. Salvos and Vinnies occasionally have card collections donated. It's a lottery but I've scored Base Set cards at op shops for 50c each.
  • Train access matters. CBD shops (Good Games, Games Lab) are the easiest to reach by PT. Cherry in Springvale is on the Pakenham/Cranbourne line.
  • Release day strategy: For major drops, Cherry Collectables and Good Games CBD get the most stock. Line up early or pre-order.

Melbourne's card scene is thriving and there's genuinely something for everyone — whether you're a competitive player, a sealed collector, a singles hunter, or just someone who likes ripping packs on a Saturday arvo. Get out there and explore. 🏙️
