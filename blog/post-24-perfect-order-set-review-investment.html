Mega Evolution: Perfect Order — Set Review & Investment Guide

March 26, 2026 · 8 min read · Card Collection Club AU

Pokemon TCG's second Mega Evolution expansion drops March 27, 2026 and it's a big one. Perfect Order brings Mega Zygarde front and centre alongside 120+ cards packed with Illustration Rares, Special Art Rares, and the continuation of the Legends Z-A storyline. Here's everything you need to know about what to chase, what to hold, and whether the sealed products are worth stashing.

Set Overview

Perfect Order is the follow-up to Ascended Heroes and continues the Mega Evolution theme that kicked off 2026. With Mega Zygarde as the headline card, this set leans heavily into Dragon and Ground-type Pokemon with a Kalos flavour throughout.

  • Total cards: 120+ (main set)
  • Secret Rares: Expected 15-20 based on JP set data
  • Key mechanic: Mega Evolution EX (no Rule Box drawback on select cards)
  • Tie-in: Pokemon Legends: Z-A (the video game driving this entire era)

Top 5 Cards to Chase

1. Mega Zygarde EX (Special Art Rare)

🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥

The cover card and the centrepiece of the set. Zygarde Complete Forme has massive collector appeal and this is the first time it's appeared as a Mega Evolution. Expect this to be the $80-150+ chase card at launch, with grading potential pushing it higher long-term.

2. Tyrunt Full Art Promo (ETB Exclusive)

🔥🔥🔥🔥

The ETB promo is a full-art Tyrunt, which has a surprisingly dedicated fanbase. ETB promos from popular sets tend to appreciate once the product goes out of print. Hold these sealed if you can.

3. Mega Gardevoir EX (Illustration Rare)

🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥

Gardevoir is consistently one of the most popular Pokemon for collectors. Any Illustration Rare featuring Gardevoir has historically held or gained value. This will likely be the sleeper hit of the set.

4. Mega Lucario EX (Special Art Rare)

🔥🔥🔥🔥

Lucario carries strong competitive and collector demand. The Mega Evolution version with premium artwork could rival Zygarde for the top card in the set by dollar value within 6 months.

5. Mega Sceptile EX (Illustration Rare)

🔥🔥🔥

A personal favourite pick. Sceptile doesn't have the mass-market appeal of Gardevoir or Lucario, but the JP version of this card generated significant buzz. Could be undervalued at launch.

Sealed Product Breakdown

Booster Display Box

RRP: ~$175-195 AUD | Investment rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Standard 36-pack display. At RRP, booster boxes of Mega Evolution sets are solid holds. The Legends Z-A storyline is expected to drive nostalgia demand for years. Buy at RRP or below, hold sealed for 12-24 months minimum.

Elite Trainer Box

RRP: ~$79.95 AUD | Investment rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

9 packs, Tyrunt promo, sleeves, energy, dice. ETBs are the gold standard for sealed investment. They're compact, recognisable, and tend to appreciate faster than booster boxes on a percentage basis. Stock up at RRP.

Booster Bundles

RRP: ~$39.95 AUD | Investment rating: ⭐⭐⭐

6-pack bundles release April 23. Decent entry point but less collector appeal than ETBs. Buy to open, not to hold.

💡 Investment Tip: Compare Perfect Order to Ascended Heroes release pricing. If Ascended Heroes ETBs are already trading above RRP (which they are), Perfect Order ETBs at retail price are essentially free money for sealed collectors. The entire Mega Evolution era will carry nostalgia premium once Legends Z-A drops.

AU Market Outlook

Australian retail allocation for Perfect Order looks healthy based on preorder availability. Major retailers (EB Games, Big W, JB Hi-Fi) all had preorders open, and local game stores report decent allocation. This means:

  • Short-term: Prices will dip below RRP in the first 2-4 weeks as supply floods the market
  • Medium-term (3-6 months): Once print runs end, sealed products will start climbing
  • Long-term (12+ months): If the Legends Z-A video game is a hit (likely), this entire era becomes highly collectible

The Bottom Line

Perfect Order is a strong mid-size set with excellent collector cards and solid sealed investment potential. It won't be the biggest set of 2026 (that honour likely goes to the 30th Anniversary set in September), but it's a reliable hold at RRP.

Our recommendation: Buy 2-4 ETBs at retail. Open one, hold the rest. Chase the Mega Zygarde SAR and Mega Gardevoir IR as singles if you can find them under $100 AUD in the first month.

Prices and availability based on AU market data as of March 2026. Not financial advice — do your own research before investing in sealed product.
