OP-16 The Time of Battle: Paramount War Investment Preview

April 4, 2026 · 8 min read · Card Collection Club

The Paramount War is coming to your card binder. OP-16 "The Time of Battle" drops in English on June 12, 2026, and it might be the most hype One Piece set since Romance Dawn. Here's what we know and how to position yourself.

What We Know So Far

OP-16 covers the Impel Down and Marineford arcs, arguably the most beloved saga in all of One Piece. The Japanese release is expected around May 30, with the English version following two weeks later on June 12.

Key details confirmed so far:

  • Paramount War theme with characters from Impel Down and Marineford
  • A new Leader card featuring a "highly influential character from the war" with new strategies
  • Expected to follow the standard booster box format (24 packs per box)
  • Japanese release ~May 30, English release June 12, 2026

Why This Set Matters for Investors

Marineford is to One Piece what the Namek Saga is to Dragon Ball. It's the emotional peak of pre-timeskip One Piece, featuring Whitebeard, Ace, Akainu, and some of the most iconic moments in anime history. Cards depicting these scenes will carry enormous demand.

Historical pattern: Sets tied to beloved story arcs consistently outperform generic expansion sets. OP-01 Romance Dawn (which introduced the game) and OP-05 Awakening of the New Era both showed strong post-release appreciation.

Cards to Watch For

While the full card list hasn't dropped yet, based on the Paramount War theme expect chase cards featuring:

  • Whitebeard (Edward Newgate) - The strongest man alive. Any Secret Rare or Manga Rare of Whitebeard will be an instant chase card.
  • Portgas D. Ace - Fan favourite with massive emotional weight from this arc. Expect premium alternate arts.
  • Akainu / Sakazuki - The villain of Marineford. Competitive viability could drive prices.
  • Monkey D. Luffy (Paramount War) - Luffy's desperate charge at Marineford is iconic imagery.
  • New Leader card - Bandai teased a new Leader with new strategies. Could be Whitebeard, Sengoku, or even Ivankov.

Supply Warning

The OP-01 Comparison

For context on what beloved-arc sets can do: OP-01 Romance Dawn Blue Bottom boxes are now selling for $5,600-$5,900 USD. Even standard English boxes sit around $1,460 USD. The Paramount War has similar "first of its kind" energy for this particular story arc.

That said, print runs are much larger now than OP-01's initial wave. Don't expect identical appreciation, but the theme alone justifies a buy-and-hold position on sealed product.

Timeline

  • Now - May 2026: Lock in preorders at best available prices
  • May 30: Japanese release - early card reveals and pricing signals
  • June 12: English release day
  • June-July: Singles market settles, identify undervalued chase cards

Bottom Line

OP-16 is a must-buy set for One Piece collectors. The Paramount War arc is too iconic to skip. Preorder boxes now, watch JP release for pricing signals, and have cash ready for singles in the first week of English release when panic sellers undervalue their pulls.

CCC recommendation: Preorder 2-4 boxes minimum. Hold 1-2 sealed for 12+ months. Open the rest and chase the Manga Rares while the set is fresh.
