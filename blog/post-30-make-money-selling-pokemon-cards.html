How to Make Money Selling Pokemon Cards in Australia

By Ian N · Feb 10, 2026 · Card Collection Club

I've been selling Pokemon cards in Australia for years and consistently pull $1-3K per month in side income. Here's how you can too — no BS, just the methods that actually work.

Method 1: Buy Low, Sell High (Flipping)

The bread and butter. The key is knowing the market better than the person selling to you.

  • Buy at conventions — vendors at card shows often buy at 70-80% of market to maintain cash flow. You can buy their "bulk rares" at 60-70% and flip on eBay at full market price.
  • Facebook Marketplace finds — people list collections without knowing what they have. I've bought $500 collections for $100 because the seller just wanted them gone.
  • Japanese product arbitrage — JP booster boxes are cheaper than English. Certain JP cards sell for more than their English equivalents. Learn the market.

Method 2: PSA Grading for Profit

This is where the real margins live. A raw card worth $50 can become $200+ in a PSA 10 slab.

The formula:

  1. Find cards where PSA 10 value is 3x+ the raw price
  2. Buy the cleanest raw copies you can find
  3. Submit to PSA (use group submissions to save on shipping)
  4. Sell the PSA 10s, keep or re-sell the 9s

Budget $40-50 AUD per card for grading. Only worth it on cards valued $60+ raw.

Method 3: Convention Selling

Card conventions are gold mines. You can do $1-3K in a single weekend.

  • Table costs: $150-500 depending on the event
  • What sells: Graded slabs, sealed product, binder singles, bulk lots
  • Tips: Have clear pricing, accept card payments (Square reader), bring business cards
  • Events: Cardcon, TCGXPO, Trading Card Summit, Collector Con — check our card shows calendar

Method 4: eBay AU

Still the best platform for selling individual cards in Australia.

  • List with clear photos (front, back, close-up of any flaws)
  • Use auction format for chase cards (competition drives price up)
  • Buy It Now for everything else with competitive pricing
  • Factor in eBay fees (~13%) and shipping when calculating margins
  • Ship in penny sleeve + toploader + bubble mailer with tracking

Method 5: Portfolio Leverage

This is the advanced play. Build a portfolio of valuable cards, track their market values, and sell when prices spike. Re-buy during dips at 5-20% below what you sold for. Rinse and repeat.

Realistic Income Expectations

  • Casual (5-10 hrs/month): $200-500/month
  • Side hustle (10-20 hrs/month): $500-2,000/month
  • Serious business (20+ hrs/month): $2,000-5,000+/month

Common Mistakes

  1. Grading everything — only grade cards with clear 3x+ ROI potential
  2. Holding too long — take profits when they're there
  3. Ignoring fees — eBay + PayPal + shipping adds up fast
  4. Buying at retail to flip — margins are too thin. Buy below market.

Cards are a legitimate side income if you treat it like a business. Track your costs, know your margins, and don't get emotional about cardboard. 💰
