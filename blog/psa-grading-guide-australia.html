PSA Grading Guide for Australian Collectors

I
Ian Nguyen
February 3, 2026 · 14 min read

PSA grading from Australia isn't straightforward. You're shipping cards to California, dealing with USD pricing, navigating long turnaround times, and paying international return shipping. But when it's done right, grading can double or triple the value of your best cards. I've submitted over 500 cards to PSA over the past four years — here's everything I've learned.

What is PSA Grading?

PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator) is the most recognised third-party grading company in the world. They examine your card's condition across four categories — centering, corners, edges, and surface — and assign a grade from 1 (Poor) to 10 (Gem Mint). The card is then sealed in a tamper-proof slab with a certification number that can be verified on PSA's website.

A PSA 10 grade means the card is virtually perfect. A PSA 9 means it's near-mint with minor imperfections visible under magnification. The price difference between a 9 and a 10 can be enormous — sometimes 3-5x for popular cards.

How to Submit — Two Paths

Option 1: Direct Submission to PSA

You can submit directly to PSA by creating an account at psacard.com and shipping your cards to their California facility.

Pros:

  • Full control over the process
  • Direct communication with PSA if issues arise
  • No middleman markup

Cons:

  • You handle all international shipping logistics
  • Customs declarations can be confusing
  • Return shipping from US to AU is expensive
  • If your package gets lost or damaged in transit, it's your problem
  • Minimum submission of 1 card (no volume discount)

How to ship direct:

  1. Create a PSA account and submission order online
  2. Print your submission form
  3. Package cards in penny sleeves → toploaders → secured with painter's tape (never rubber bands)
  4. Place in a rigid bubble mailer or small box with padding
  5. Ship via Australia Post International Registered with tracking and insurance
  6. Declare accurately on customs form — "Collectible Trading Cards for Grading Service"
  7. Wait. And wait some more.

Option 2: Use a Local Middleman (Recommended)

This is what most Australian collectors do, and honestly, it's what I recommend for most people. Local middleman services collect cards from multiple Australian customers, bulk ship to PSA, and handle all the logistics.

🇦🇺 Australian PSA Middlemen

Graded Gem — One of the most established Australian grading middlemen. Based in Melbourne. They handle PSA, BGS, and CGC submissions. Pricing is transparent — you pay PSA's fee plus a per-card handling fee (usually $10-15 AUD). They insure the shipment and handle customs. Regular group submissions every 2-4 weeks.

Lux Cards — Sydney-based middleman with a strong reputation. Similar model to Graded Gem — bulk submissions with handling fee. They also offer a "pre-grade" service where they assess your cards before submission and advise on likely grades. Useful for deciding whether to submit borderline cards.

Cherry Collectables — The big card retailer also offers grading submission services from their Springvale (Melbourne) store. Convenient if you're already buying product from them. Pricing is competitive for their members.

Local card shops — Some Good Games and independent stores run group submissions. Ask your local shop — the handling fee is usually lower and you're supporting the local community.

Pros of middlemen:

  • They handle all international shipping, customs, and insurance
  • Bulk shipping reduces per-card shipping costs significantly
  • Many offer volume discounts on handling fees
  • Some offer pre-grading assessment
  • Returns come to a local address — no dealing with USPS/AusPost international issues

Cons:

  • Small per-card handling fee ($10-15 AUD typically)
  • You're on their submission schedule, not yours
  • Adds time to the overall turnaround
  • Your cards are in someone else's hands

Turnaround Times — The Reality

PSA's stated turnaround times are business days from when they receive and process your submission. For Australian collectors, the total timeline looks more like this:

  • Value tier (total): 5-8 months
  • Economy tier (total): 3-6 months
  • Regular tier (total): 2-4 months
  • Express tier (total): 3-5 weeks

These are realistic estimates including shipping both ways, customs processing, PSA intake queue, and middleman handling time. If you need cards back quickly, you're paying Express minimum.

Factor turnaround time into your investment calculations. A card that takes 6 months to come back might have changed in value significantly. The market doesn't wait for your slabs.

Tips for Maximizing Your Grades

After 500+ submissions, here's what I've learned about getting the best possible grades:

Centering

  • PSA's centering standard for a 10 is 60/40 front and 75/25 back
  • Use a centering tool or app — "PSA Centering" apps are available for iPhone and Android
  • Check both front AND back. Many people forget the back
  • If centering is 55/45 or worse on the front, you're likely looking at a PSA 9 maximum
  • Japanese cards generally have better centering than English

Surface

  • Use a jeweller's loupe (10x) to check the surface under direct light
  • Look for: scratches, print lines, silvering on edges, roller marks, ink spots
  • Modern full arts and rainbow rares scratch incredibly easily — handle with clean hands only
  • NEVER clean a card. No microfibre cloth, no rubbing, no "restoring." PSA can detect cleaning and it results in altered grades or rejection
  • Pack-fresh to sleeve is the ideal chain of custody. The fewer times a card is handled, the better

Corners

  • Check all four corners with a loupe
  • Even micro-whitening on one corner can drop you from a 10 to a 9
  • Factory whitening (from the cutting process) is common and PSA does account for it, but excessive whitening is still penalised
  • Japanese cards typically have less corner whitening due to better quality control

Edges

  • Run your finger along all four edges — feel for any rough spots or nicks
  • Check under magnification for edge whitening, especially on dark-bordered cards
  • Edge damage is common from poor pack opening technique — always open packs carefully

When NOT to Grade

Grading isn't always the right call. Here's when to skip it:

  1. The raw card is worth less than $50 AUD. At Economy tier ($75-100 all-in), you need significant grade premium to break even. Cards under $50 rarely have enough spread between raw and PSA 10 to justify the cost.
  2. You can see visible damage. If there's whitening, scratches, or dents visible to the naked eye, you're looking at a PSA 7-8 at best. The premium for PSA 7-8 over raw is usually negligible. Save your money.
  3. The card has high PSA 10 population. When 10,000+ copies are graded PSA 10, the premium shrinks because supply is abundant. Check PSA's pop report before submitting.
  4. You want to sell quickly. The 3-6 month turnaround means your capital is locked up. If you need liquidity, sell raw.
  5. It's a bulk modern card. Just because you pulled a holo Pidgeot from the latest set doesn't mean it should be graded. Be honest about whether a card has grading ROI.

PSA vs BGS vs CGC — Quick Comparison

While this guide focuses on PSA, you should know the alternatives:

  • PSA — Most recognised brand, highest liquidity, most buyers trust PSA slabs. Best for Pokemon and One Piece. The go-to for most Australian collectors.
  • BGS (Beckett) — Sub-grades give more detail (centering, edges, corners, surface scored separately). Popular with sports card collectors. A BGS 10 "Black Label" (perfect 10 in all sub-grades) is the holy grail but extremely rare. Less liquid than PSA for Pokemon.
  • CGC — Cheaper than PSA, faster turnaround, good reputation growing. The slab design is modern and clean. Gaining popularity in Australia but still trades at a discount vs PSA for the same grade. Good option for mid-value cards where PSA's premium isn't justified.

Packaging Your Cards for Submission

How you package cards matters. Poor packaging = damage in transit = lower grades or worse, lost cards.

  1. Penny sleeve first. Card goes face-down into a standard penny sleeve.
  2. Then into a toploader. Semi-rigid Card Saver 1 holders are preferred by PSA. Regular toploaders work too.
  3. Secure with painter's tape. A small piece across the top of the Card Saver to prevent the card from sliding out. NEVER use regular tape — it can leave residue on the card.
  4. Stack and wrap. Stack toploaders together, wrap with a rubber band AROUND the stack (not touching the cards), and place in a Team Bag or ziplock.
  5. Box with padding. Place everything in a rigid box with bubble wrap or foam padding. The package should not shift when shaken.
  6. Label clearly. Include your PSA submission form inside the package and mark the outside as fragile.

The PSA Grading Process (What Happens to Your Card)

  1. Intake: PSA receives and logs your submission
  2. Research: Card identity is verified against their database
  3. Authentication: Card is checked for authenticity (fakes get flagged and returned)
  4. Grading: A trained grader evaluates centering, corners, edges, and surface
  5. Quality check: A second grader reviews borderline calls
  6. Encapsulation: Card is sealed in the PSA slab with grade label
  7. Shipping: Returned to you or your middleman

Final Tips

  • Grade in batches. Shipping costs are largely fixed whether you send 5 or 50 cards. Accumulate and submit in bulk.
  • Use a middleman for your first time. Learn the process before going direct.
  • Keep a spreadsheet. Track every submission — card, cost, service tier, date sent, date received, grade, and resulting value. This data will make your future decisions much better.
  • Don't grade emotionally. Grade for ROI, not because you love the card. Your beautiful Gyarados V doesn't need a slab if the PSA 10 is worth $30.
  • Check PSA's pop reports before submitting. Low population = higher premium. High population = don't bother unless the card is genuinely valuable.

Grading is a game within a game. Master it and you'll add serious value to your collection. Approach it carelessly and you'll bleed money on fees for cards that didn't need slabs. Be strategic, be patient, and grade smart. 🏆
