One Piece TCG Market Report: February 2026

M
Michael H
February 15, 2026 · 10 min read

The One Piece TCG continues to be the most exciting card game on the market right now. While Pokemon remains the king of nostalgia collecting, One Piece is where the action is for competitive players and speculators alike. Here's what's happening in February 2026.

OP10 Royal Blood — The Set That Changed Everything

OP10 Royal Blood dropped in Japan in late January and the English release is confirmed for late March. This set has completely shaken up the competitive landscape with new Celestial Dragon leader cards and the introduction of the Holy Knight archetype. The impact on secondary market prices has been significant.

The Japanese release has given us a preview of what's coming, and the hype is fully justified. The manga art secret rares in OP10 are some of the best we've seen — the Shanks manga art SR in particular has been commanding ¥25,000+ ($250 AUD) on the JP market, which typically translates to higher EN prices upon release.

Shanks Manga Art SR (OP10)

The flagship card of Royal Blood. Bandai went all-in with the artwork on this one — it's a faithful recreation of the iconic manga panel where Shanks stops Akainu at Marineford. JP copies are already at $250 AUD and climbing. Pre-orders for EN copies are hitting $300+ on speculation. If the EN pull rate mirrors OP09, this will be the most expensive One Piece card in the modern era.

Gear 5 Luffy SR (OP05)

This card refuses to die. Every time I think it's peaked, it climbs another 10%. The Gear 5 reveal in the anime drove initial demand, and now with the live-action Season 2 confirmed for mid-2026, anything Luffy-related is getting a boost. $320 AUD and climbing — I wouldn't be surprised to see $400 by mid-year.

Zoro Alt Art Leader (OP09)

The OP09 alt art leaders were a game-changer for the TCG. Zoro's alt art features the King of Hell three-sword style and it's genuinely one of the best card artworks I've ever seen across any TCG. $180 and trending up — this is a strong long-term hold.

Nami SP (OP01)

Nami has been on a slow decline since hitting her peak of $450 in late 2025. At $380 she's still extremely valuable, but the trend is clear. The introduction of newer waifu-style SP cards in OP09 and OP10 is distributing demand across more products. She'll likely stabilise around $320-350.

Rob Lucci Alt Art Leader (OP05)

Competitive meta shifts are hurting Lucci. The OP10 meta is shaping up to be unfavourable for Black-heavy builds, and competitive demand was a major price driver. Pure collector appeal is keeping it above $50, but don't expect a bounce unless the meta shifts back.

JP vs EN Price Gaps

The JP-EN price gap remains one of the best arbitrage opportunities in the TCG space. Here are the current gaps worth noting:

  • Gear 5 Luffy SR: JP ¥20,000 ($200 AUD) vs EN $320 AUD — 60% EN premium
  • Shanks Manga Art: JP ¥25,000 ($250) — EN pre-orders at $300+ — expect EN to settle 30-50% higher
  • Yamato SP: JP ¥6,000 ($60) vs EN $95 — 58% EN premium
  • OP09 Booster Box: JP ¥6,500 ($65) vs EN $130 — exactly 2x

The EN premium is driven by lower print runs and higher demand in Western markets. For collectors happy with JP cards, there's genuine value in buying Japanese. The card quality is typically better too — thicker stock, more consistent printing.

Pro tip: If you're buying JP singles, check Yahoo Auctions Japan via a proxy service like Buyee or Sendico. Prices are consistently 10-15% cheaper than what Japanese cards sell for on eBay AU.

Sealed Product Trends

Booster Boxes

EN booster boxes have been on an interesting trajectory. OP01 Romance Dawn boxes have stabilised around $450 AUD — they're not going up anymore but they're not coming down either. The floor seems firm. OP02-OP04 boxes are the "forgotten middle children" trading between $100-130 with minimal movement.

The real action is in OP05 and OP06 sealed. OP05 boxes have climbed to $180 on the back of Gear 5 Luffy demand, and OP06 is sneaking up to $145 as people realise it's one of the last "cheap" sets with genuinely good chase cards.

Starter Decks

Don't sleep on sealed starter decks. The ST-01 through ST-04 starter decks (especially ST-01 Straw Hat Crew) have tripled in price over the past year. ST-01 is sitting at $80 AUD sealed — up from $25 at retail. New player demand drives these and One Piece is still onboarding players at a massive rate.

February Outlook

The market is in a "pre-OP10 EN" holding pattern. Smart money is accumulating JP OP10 singles now before the EN release creates a price spike. The live-action Season 2 announcement has put a floor under all premium Luffy and Straw Hat cards.

My picks for February:

  • Buy: JP OP10 manga art SRs — the EN markup will be significant
  • Buy: OP05/OP06 sealed boxes — quietly climbing and undervalued
  • Hold: Gear 5 Luffy, Zoro alt art leader — both have momentum
  • Sell/Take profits: Nami SP if you're sitting on gains — downtrend is clear
  • Avoid: EN OP10 pre-orders at current inflated prices — wait for the release week dip

The One Piece TCG isn't going anywhere. Bandai's tournament support, the anime/manga/live-action content pipeline, and genuine gameplay quality make this the best TCG investment thesis outside of Pokemon. Stay sharp out there. 🏴‍☠️
