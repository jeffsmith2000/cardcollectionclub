PSA vs CGC vs BGS — Which Grading Company is Best?

By John C · February 18, 2026 · 9 min read

Grading your cards is one of the most important decisions you'll make as a collector or investor. A high grade from a reputable company can multiply a card's value many times over — but choosing the wrong service, or submitting at the wrong time, can leave you out of pocket with months of waiting.

For Australian collectors, the decision comes with extra considerations: international shipping costs, customs declarations, and turnaround times that can stretch even longer than what our American mates deal with. Let's break down the big three — PSA, CGC, and BGS — and help you figure out which one suits your collection best.

PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator)

The Good

  • Market dominance: PSA is the undisputed king of resale value. A PSA 10 consistently commands a premium over equivalent grades from CGC or BGS across almost every card.
  • Brand recognition: Even casual collectors know the red label. It's the most universally trusted and recognised slab.
  • Population reports: PSA's pop reports are the most comprehensive, giving you clear data on how many copies exist at each grade.
  • Clean aesthetic: The label design is simple and elegant. Buyers love it.

The Bad

  • Cost from Australia: Base service starts at ~$25 USD per card (~$38 AUD), but by the time you factor in shipping both ways, insurance, and customs, you're looking at $60–$80 AUD per card minimum for bulk submissions.
  • Turnaround: Economy service can take 6–12 months. Express tiers exist but are expensive ($75–$300 USD per card).
  • No sub-grades: You get a single number. No breakdown of centering, surface, edges, or corners.
  • Grading consistency: PSA has faced criticism for inconsistency, particularly during their pandemic-era backlog.
Best For: High-value cards where resale is the primary goal. Vintage Pokémon, trophy cards, and anything worth $200+ AUD raw.

CGC (Certified Guaranty Company)

The Good

  • Sub-grades included: Every card gets centering, surface, corners, and edges scores. Great for transparency.
  • Competitive pricing: Starts at ~$15 USD per card (~$23 AUD) for economy, making it the most affordable option.
  • Faster turnaround: Generally 4–8 months for economy, with quicker options available.
  • Growing acceptance: CGC has made huge inroads in the Pokémon and One Piece TCG markets.
  • Australian-friendly: Several Aussie middleman services now offer CGC bulk submission at reduced rates.

The Bad

  • Lower resale premium: CGC 10s generally sell for 20–40% less than PSA 10s on the same card.
  • Perfect 10 vs Pristine 10: CGC uses a "Perfect 10" and "Pristine 10" distinction that can confuse buyers.
  • Newer to cards: CGC comes from the comics world and only entered card grading in 2020.
Best For: Mid-range cards ($30–$200 AUD raw), collectors who want sub-grade detail, and budget-conscious submitters.

BGS (Beckett Grading Services)

The Good

  • The Black Label: A BGS Black Label 10 (Pristine with all four sub-grades at 10) is arguably the most prestigious grade in the hobby. It commands massive premiums.
  • Sub-grades: Like CGC, BGS provides centering, surface, corners, and edges breakdowns.
  • Sports card pedigree: BGS is the gold standard in sports cards and carries serious credibility.

The Bad

  • Declining Pokémon relevance: BGS has lost significant market share to PSA and CGC in the Pokémon space.
  • Expensive: Similar pricing to PSA but without the same Pokémon resale premium.
  • Turnaround issues: BGS has struggled with backlogs and customer service in recent years.
  • Limited One Piece presence: Very few collectors submit One Piece TCG to BGS.
Best For: Sports card collectors, or Pokémon cards where you're confident of a Black Label 10.

Tips for Aussie Submitters

  1. Use a middleman service: Companies like Graded Gem, Cardboard Grading, and local Facebook groups run bulk submissions that drastically reduce per-card shipping costs.
  2. Declare accurately for customs: Undervaluing cards on customs forms can lead to seizure. Be honest — it's not worth the risk.
  3. Insure everything: International shipping both ways means double the risk. Always insure at replacement value.
  4. Grade selectively: At $40–$80 AUD per card, only submit cards where the graded value significantly exceeds raw value plus grading costs.
  5. Consider the market: If you're selling locally in Australia, CGC is perfectly acceptable and saves you money. If you're selling internationally on eBay, PSA will fetch you more.

Our Verdict

For most Australian Pokémon and One Piece TCG collectors, CGC offers the best value proposition. The sub-grades add transparency, the cost is lower, turnaround is faster, and the resale gap is narrowing. However, for truly high-value cards — your Base Set Charizards, your Illustrator Pikachus, your trophy cards — PSA remains the smart choice for maximising resale.

BGS is a harder sell in 2026 unless you're deep in the sports card world or chasing that elusive Black Label.

Whichever service you choose, grade wisely, submit patiently, and always protect your cards in transit. Good luck, legends.
