Psyduck Munch PSA 9 Has Doubled: Why This Japanese Promo Is Surging

April 2, 2026 · 6 min read · Card Collection Club

If you've been sleeping on the Pokemon x Munch collaboration promos, it's time to wake up. The Psyduck Munch PSA 9 (#286/SM-P) has nearly doubled in value since late 2025, with recent sales hitting $2,260 USD on average. Here's what's driving the surge and what it means for collectors.

The Numbers

📈 Psyduck Munch PSA 9 — Price History

Late 2025: $916 – $1,125 USD

March 2026 (14-day avg): $2,260 USD

Recent sales: $1,710 – $2,626 USD

Raw NM: $1,069 USD (up 90%+ in 30 days)

Appreciation: ~100% in ~4 months

That's not a typo. The Psyduck Munch PSA 9 has roughly doubled from its late-2025 lows. Even raw near-mint copies have surged over 90% in the last 30 days alone.

Why Is It Surging?

1. Art Collaboration Scarcity
The Pokemon x Munch collaboration from 2018 was a limited Japanese promotion featuring five cards with artwork inspired by Edvard Munch's "The Scream." These were only available through a lottery at the Tokyo Metropolitan Art Museum. You couldn't just walk into a shop and buy them — they were genuinely limited.

2. Cross-Collectible Appeal
Art collaboration cards attract buyers who aren't typical Pokemon collectors. Art enthusiasts, museum memorabilia collectors, and crossover hobbyists all compete for these cards. That broadens the buyer pool significantly.

3. The "Forgotten Promo" Effect
After the initial hype in 2018-2019, Munch promos quietly consolidated in price for years. Many collectors forgot about them or moved on to newer releases. Now, as the Pokemon vintage market matures, investors are rediscovering undervalued Japanese promos — and the Munch series tops the list.

4. PSA Population
The PSA 9 population for Psyduck Munch is relatively small. High-grade copies are scarce, and as more collectors want them, supply simply can't keep up with demand.

The Full Munch Collection

Five cards were released in the collaboration:

  • Pikachu (#288/SM-P) — The most expensive of the set, PSA 10s regularly exceeding $10,000 USD
  • Eevee (#287/SM-P) — Strong demand, driven by Eeveelution collectors
  • Psyduck (#286/SM-P) — The one surging hardest right now
  • Mimikyu (#289/SM-P) — Fan-favourite Pokemon with cult following
  • Rowlet (#290/SM-P) — Most affordable entry point

Collector's Note: Psyduck has always been a beloved "meme" Pokemon with broad appeal beyond competitive players. The Munch artwork captures this perfectly — Psyduck's perpetual headache paired with "The Scream" is iconic. That cultural resonance drives long-term value.

Is It Too Late to Buy?

Depends on your timeframe. If you're looking for a quick flip at current prices, you're chasing momentum — risky. But if you believe in 3-5 year appreciation for unique Pokemon art promos, the Munch series still has room to grow.

For context, the Pikachu Munch PSA 10 went from ~$5,000 in 2021 to over $10,000+ in 2026. The Psyduck at $2,200 still feels underpriced relative to the Pikachu, especially considering the artwork is arguably more memorable.

⚠️ Risk Factors

Japanese promos can be volatile. Counterfeits exist in the market — always buy PSA-graded or from trusted sellers. And remember: past performance doesn't guarantee future returns. Don't invest money you can't afford to hold for 2+ years.

For Australian Collectors

At current exchange rates, a PSA 9 Psyduck Munch will cost roughly $3,400-$3,900 AUD. That's a significant investment, but for a card with genuine scarcity, cultural significance, and proven price trajectory, it's one of the stronger plays in the Pokemon market right now.
