One Piece TCG Beginner's Guide — Everything You Need to Know

By John C · Feb 18, 2026 · Card Collection Club

One Piece TCG is the hottest card game on the planet right now. If you're a Pokemon collector eyeing those One Piece cards at your local shop, or an anime fan wanting to get into the TCG — this guide covers everything you need to start.

Why One Piece TCG?

Bandai launched the One Piece Card Game in 2022 and it's grown faster than any TCG in history. The art quality is insane, the gameplay is strategic but accessible, and the collecting community is exploding — especially in Australia.

Key reasons to get in now:

  • Early sets (OP01-OP03) are already appreciating fast
  • The anime is at its peak popularity (Gear 5 arc)
  • Lower entry cost than Pokemon — you can build competitive decks for $50-100
  • Sealed product is actually available (unlike Pokemon's constant shortages)

Which Starter Deck Should I Buy?

Bandai releases starter decks with every new wave. For 2026, here are the best starting points:

  • ST-13 (The Three Brothers) — Great value, fan-favourite characters
  • ST-14 (3D2Y) — Strong competitive foundation
  • Any starter deck featuring your favourite character — you'll enjoy collecting more

Starter decks cost $20-25 AUD and come ready to play out of the box.

Where to Buy in Australia

You've got plenty of options:

  • Local card shops — check our directory of 100+ Australian shops
  • Good Games — chain stores across most capital cities
  • eBay AU — best for singles and older products
  • Gameology / Toymate / Zing — for sealed product at retail

What to Collect

There are two main approaches:

Competitive Players

Focus on leaders and staple cards for your deck archetype. Join local tournaments at your nearest card shop — most run weekly One Piece events.

Collectors

Chase the alt arts, manga arts, and secret rares. The art in this game is genuinely museum-quality. Key collectible cards: Shanks Manga Art (OP01), Luffy Gear 5 Leader (OP05), Yamato SEC (OP04), Boa Hancock Alt Art (OP09).

Tips for New Collectors

  1. Sleeve everything — even commons. Get penny sleeves + toploaders for anything valuable
  2. Learn the rarity symbols — C (Common), UC, R, SR (Super Rare), SEC (Secret), L (Leader), Manga/Alt Art
  3. Track prices on eBay AU — it's the most accurate source for Australian market values
  4. Join the community — Facebook groups, Discord servers, local shop events
  5. Buy sealed product you'll open AND enjoy — don't just speculate on boxes you won't touch

The One Piece TCG community in Australia is genuinely welcoming. Hit up your local shop, grab a starter deck, and start pulling. Welcome aboard, nakama. ⛵
