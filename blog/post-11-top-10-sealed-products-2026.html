Top 10 Sealed Products to Hold in 2026

By Ian N · February 18, 2026 · 8 min read

If you've been collecting sealed product for any length of time, you'll know the golden rule: buy smart, hold patiently, and let nostalgia do the heavy lifting. 2026 is shaping up to be one of the best years in recent memory for sealed product investing, with several sets reaching the end of their print runs and demand continuing to climb across both Pokémon and One Piece TCG.

Here at Card Collection Club, we've spent months tracking market prices, print run data, and community sentiment to compile our top 10 sealed products worth holding right now. Whether you're just starting your sealed vault or looking to add a few more gems, these picks should be on your radar.

1. Pokémon 151 Booster Bundle (Japanese)

The Japanese Pokémon 151 booster bundle has already seen significant appreciation since its June 2023 release, climbing from around ¥5,400 to roughly $120–$140 AUD on the secondary market. The Kanto nostalgia factor here is unmatched, and the Japanese print quality remains superior. With the Master Ball reverse holo pattern exclusive to this set, demand shows no sign of slowing.

Current Price: ~$130 AUD | 12-Month Target: $180–$220 AUD

2. Pokémon Scarlet & Violet — Obsidian Flames Booster Box

Obsidian Flames flew under the radar at launch, overshadowed by 151 hype. But the Charizard ex (Art Rare #215) has anchored this set's value, and booster boxes are sitting around $160–$175 AUD — well below replacement cost for many retailers. As a base Scarlet & Violet era set, it will appreciate once the generation cycle ends.

Current Price: ~$170 AUD | 12-Month Target: $220–$260 AUD

3. One Piece TCG OP-01 Romance Dawn Booster Box (English)

The OG. Romance Dawn booster boxes in English have been climbing steadily, and we're now looking at $350–$400 AUD per box. The Shanks (Manga Art), Luffy Leader cards, and the sheer significance of being the first English set make this a cornerstone holding. If One Piece TCG follows even half the trajectory of Pokémon, this box is generational.

Current Price: ~$380 AUD | 12-Month Target: $500+ AUD

4. Pokémon Crown Zenith Elite Trainer Box

Crown Zenith was the swan song for the Sword & Shield era, packed with Galarian Gallery hits and some of the most beautiful art in the modern TCG. ETBs were printed heavily, but absorption has been strong. Currently hovering around $85–$95 AUD, these are a solid medium-term hold.

Current Price: ~$90 AUD | 12-Month Target: $120–$140 AUD

5. One Piece TCG OP-05 Awakening of the New Era (Japanese)

OP-05 introduced some of the most iconic manga art cards in the One Piece TCG, including the stunning Luffy Gear 5 alternate art. Japanese boxes are hovering around $110–$130 AUD, a genuine bargain for what's considered one of the best sets in the franchise. The anime's continued global momentum only helps.

Current Price: ~$120 AUD | 12-Month Target: $170–$200 AUD

6. Pokémon Surging Sparks Booster Box

The latest Scarlet & Violet era flagship set has delivered incredible pull rates and a deep pool of desirable cards. At current retail of $170–$185 AUD per booster box, we recommend stashing a couple while they're still in print. The Pikachu ex Special Art Rare alone could carry this set long-term.

Current Price: ~$180 AUD | 12-Month Target: $230–$270 AUD

7. Pokémon Evolving Skies Booster Box

Evolving Skies remains the gold standard of modern Pokémon sealed product. Booster boxes have already surged past $550 AUD and continue climbing. The Eeveelution alternate arts — particularly Umbreon VMAX (#215) — are some of the most desirable modern cards ever printed. If you've got one sealed, you're sitting on a gem.

Current Price: ~$560 AUD | 12-Month Target: $700+ AUD

8. One Piece TCG OP-03 Pillars of Strength (English)

OP-03 introduced fan-favourite characters like Portgas D. Ace and Yamato into the English TCG. Booster boxes are around $130–$150 AUD and represent excellent value given the set's competitive relevance and collector appeal. The Ace manga art alternate art has been a consistent seller.

Current Price: ~$140 AUD | 12-Month Target: $190–$220 AUD

9. Pokémon Paldea Evolved Booster Box

Paldea Evolved brought us some stunning Illustration Rare and Special Art Rare cards, including the beloved Lechonk and the striking Iono SAR. Boxes are criminally cheap at $150–$165 AUD. This is a set that will be looked back on fondly once the SV era concludes.

Current Price: ~$160 AUD | 12-Month Target: $200–$240 AUD

10. Pokémon Prismatic Evolutions Elite Trainer Box

The newest entry on our list, Prismatic Evolutions launched with enormous hype and has already proven difficult to keep on shelves. Featuring the complete Eeveelution lineup with new Terastal ex forms, this set is dripping with collector appeal. ETBs at $80–$90 AUD retail are a no-brainer to stash.

Current Price: ~$85 AUD | 12-Month Target: $130–$160 AUD

Final Thoughts

Sealed product investing is a marathon, not a sprint. The best returns come from holding three to five years minimum, letting print runs dry up and nostalgia build. Diversify across both Pokémon and One Piece TCG, favour Japanese product where possible for lower print runs, and always store your sealed items in a cool, dry environment away from direct sunlight.

As always, only invest what you can afford to lock away, and remember — the best time to buy sealed was yesterday. The second best time is today.

Happy collecting, legends. 🇦🇺
