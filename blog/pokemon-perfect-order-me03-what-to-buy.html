Pokemon TCG Perfect Order (ME-03): What to Buy

By Card Collection Club | March 2026 | Set Guide

Pokemon TCG Perfect Order (ME-03) is the third entry in the Master Edition series — premium Japanese-exclusive sets that feature high-rarity reprints of iconic cards in new artwork. ME-03 focuses on the Legendary theme, bringing Gold and Silver era Legendaries back in modern immersive art treatment. Here's exactly what to buy, what to skip, and how to navigate AU pricing.

📌 Master Edition basics: ME series sets are Japanese exclusive, meaning they don't receive global EN releases. All cards from ME-03 are Japanese language. This actually increases their desirability for serious collectors — Japanese cards grade cleaner and are rarer in the AU market.

What Makes ME-03 Special

The Master Edition series was created to give collectors a premium, museum-quality version of the most beloved cards in Pokemon TCG history. ME-03 applies this treatment to Gen 2 Legendaries — Ho-Oh, Lugia, Celebi, Entei, Raikou, and Suicune — with full-art canvas-style illustrations and gold foil elements. The box design is premium: every box is a collectible item itself.

Print runs for ME sets are significantly smaller than standard booster sets, which drives higher long-term appreciation. ME-01 and ME-02 boxes have already appreciated 40–60% from release price in the AU secondary market.

Top Cards to Target

MUST HAVE

Lugia Immersive Rare (IR)

Lugia is the single most popular card in Pokemon TCG history in terms of collector demand. Any new Lugia card in a premium art treatment is an automatic high-priority target. ME-03's Lugia IR features a full-page ocean storm illustration — early preview images show it's already being called one of the best Lugia arts ever printed. AU price prediction: $300–$500 raw at release, $800–$1,200 PSA 10 at 12 months. Buy at any price under $450 raw at launch.

STRONG BUY

Ho-Oh Special Art Rare (SAR)

Ho-Oh's SAR features a sunrise motif that ties directly to the iconography of the first Johto episode — the single most nostalgic scene for a generation of Pokemon fans. Ho-Oh always carries nostalgia premium and the art quality here is exceptional. AU price: $150–$250 raw at release. More accessible than Lugia but still strong appreciation potential.

VALUE PLAY

Suicune Full Art

Suicune has a dedicated and growing collector fanbase. The full art treatment in ME-03 pairs it with the Ecruteak City setting — a popular locale among nostalgia-driven collectors. Suicune cards consistently punch above their general Legendary tier in secondary market performance. AU price target: $80–$130 raw at launch, $200–$350 PSA 10 long-term.

COLLECTOR PICK

Celebi Reverse Holo Special Print

Celebi remains one of the most popular small Legendaries for collectors, particularly for its connection to the second Pokemon movie and the nostalgia it carries. The ME-03 Celebi reverse holo special print is a small-run variant with exceptional foil treatment. Lower price point than the top IRs — a more accessible way to get a premium ME-03 piece in your collection.

Box Opening: Is It Worth It?

ME-03 box RRP in Japan: approximately ¥15,000 (~$155 AUD). By the time boxes reach AU retailers or importers, expect $180–$240 AUD per box. Pull rate analysis from ME-01 and ME-02:

  • Guaranteed: 1 high rarity per box (IR/SAR/Full Art)
  • Roughly 1-in-3 boxes: A top-tier IR hit
  • 1-in-6 boxes: A top-tier IR in gem mint condition

Box opening math: At $220 AUD per box, you're paying $220 for a random mix of value. Singles for the top 3 cards cost roughly the same as 1–1.5 boxes. Unless you enjoy the opening experience, buying singles is more efficient for value.

Where to Get ME-03 in Australia

ME-03 is Japanese-exclusive, so it won't be available at most mainstream AU card retailers. Sources for Australians:

  • Speciality Japanese import card shops: Find them on the Card Collection Club directory — filter by "Japanese imports"
  • eBay AU from Japan-based sellers: Allow 1–3 weeks for shipping. Use completed sales to check fair pricing before buying
  • Conventions: Cardcon and TCGXO often have Japanese import vendors — you can inspect condition in person
  • Impulse Buy warning: Avoid paying over 30% premium from JP RRP at launch — stock becomes more available 4–6 weeks after release as more importers bring product in

Grading Considerations

Japanese ME cards grade exceptionally well for a specific reason: the card stock is denser and the foil treatment is more durable than EN equivalents. PSA 10 rates on ME series cards from Japanese boxes are significantly higher than EN set equivalents. If you're grading, source from sealed Japanese boxes rather than singles — pre-owned singles may already have handling wear that drops them to PSA 9.

ME-03 is a strong set for both collectors and investors who approach it strategically. Prioritise Lugia and Ho-Oh, consider Suicune as a value play, and don't overpay at launch when stock becomes more available after 4–6 weeks.
