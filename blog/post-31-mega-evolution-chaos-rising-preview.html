Mega Evolution - Chaos Rising: Full Preview and What to Buy

March 31, 2026 · 9 min read · Card Collection Club AU

The Mega Evolution era continues. Chaos Rising is the second Mega Evolution set for Pokemon TCG Scarlet and Violet, dropping May 22, 2026. Here's everything we know and a clear buying strategy for AU collectors.

Set Overview

  • Set name: Mega Evolution - Chaos Rising
  • JP equivalent: Ninja Spinner
  • Release date: May 22, 2026
  • Pre-releases: May 9-17, 2026
  • Card count: 120+ cards
  • Featured Megas: Mega Greninja ex, Mega Pyroar ex, Mega Floette ex, Mega Dragalge ex

Chase Cards

Based on the Japanese Ninja Spinner release, here are the cards that will drive value:

Mega Greninja ex

The headliner. Greninja is consistently one of the most popular Pokemon for collectors and players alike. Expect the Special Art Rare to be the most expensive card in the set.

JP SAR equivalent: Already trading at a premium in Japanese.

Mega Pyroar ex

Fire-type Megas always do well. Pyroar has a loyal fanbase and the art direction on this card has been praised.

Mega Floette ex

A surprise inclusion. The Floette line has sentimental value for many fans (AZ's Floette from X&Y). Could be a sleeper hit if the artwork delivers.

Mega Dragalge ex

Poison/Dragon typing makes this unique. Niche appeal but could perform well with competitive players.

What We Know from the JP Release

Ninja Spinner released in Japan alongside Chaos Rising's EN date. Key takeaways from the JP market:

  • Pull rates for SARs (Special Art Rares) appear similar to Ascended Heroes - roughly 1 per 2-3 boxes
  • The Greninja SAR has been the clear value leader in JP
  • Box prices in JP have held steady, suggesting healthy demand
  • The set has good competitive cards, which drives sustained demand beyond just collectors

Strategy: If you can read JP prices before EN release (which you can, since JP drops same day), use them as a guide. EN prices usually start higher on release day then settle to 1.2-1.5x JP equivalent within 2-3 weeks.

Also Releasing: Abyss Eye (JP Only)

On the same day (May 22), Japan also gets Abyss Eye, featuring Mega Darkrai ex. These cards will likely appear in a future EN set later in 2026. If you import JP, Abyss Eye is worth watching for the Darkrai chase card.

AU Buying Strategy

Pre-Release (May 9-17)

Sign up at your local store now. Pre-release kits typically include 4 packs and a promo card. It's the cheapest way to crack packs from a new set, and you get to play before anyone else.

Booster Boxes

Expected AU pricing: $185-210 AUD per booster box (36 packs). If you see pre-orders under $185, that's a buy.

ETBs

Standard ETBs will run $79.95 AUD RRP but expect them to be available at RRP. Chaos Rising likely won't have the supply issues that Prismatic Evolutions had.

Hold vs Crack Decision

  • Pre-order 2-4 boxes at best available price
  • Crack 1-2 at pre-release for early market singles flipping
  • Hold 1-2 sealed if you believe in Greninja long-term
  • Buy singles 3-4 weeks after release when prices bottom

Don't FOMO

Ascended Heroes showed us that modern Pokemon sets get heavy print runs. Unless Chaos Rising has a breakout card that warps the meta, sealed appreciation will be gradual. The real money is in buying singles at their post-release dip.

Key Dates for Your Calendar

  • May 9-17: Pre-release events across AU
  • May 22: Official release day
  • May 23-24: Melbourne Pokemon TCG Regionals (Chaos Rising legal)
  • Jun 7: The TCG Expo Melbourne (good venue for singles trading)

The Melbourne Regionals happening the day after release is huge. Competitive players will be scrambling for Chaos Rising cards, which means strong Day 1 demand for playable cards. If you crack boxes at pre-release and pull competitive staples, list them fast.

The Bottom Line

Chaos Rising looks like a solid mid-year set. Greninja is a genuine chase card, the Mega Evolution theme continues to resonate, and the JP data suggests healthy demand. For AU collectors: pre-order at good prices, attend pre-release events, and have a plan for whether you're cracking, holding, or flipping.
