Best Pokemon Cards to Invest In 2026: Top 15 Growth Picks

M
Michael H
February 5, 2026 · 16 min read

Disclaimer up front: I'm a collector first, investor second. These are my personal picks based on market trends, historical patterns, and collector sentiment — not financial advice. The card market is volatile and you should never invest money you can't afford to lose. That said, if you're going to buy cards anyway, you might as well buy ones with upside potential.

Here are my 15 picks for 2026, ranked by confidence level. I've mixed vintage and modern, English and Japanese, and different price points to give everyone something to work with.

All prices are AUD, based on raw (ungraded) card prices from recent eBay AU sold listings as of early February 2026. Target prices are my 12-month estimates.

High Confidence Picks

1. Umbreon VMAX Alternate Art — Evolving Skies (#215)

High Confidence
Current: $580 AUD Target: $800-900 AUD

The modern blue-chip. Evolving Skies is out of print, supply is finite and shrinking, and Umbreon is the most popular Eeveelution. The trajectory has been consistently upward for 4+ years. This is as close to a "safe" card investment as exists. The only risk is a significant market-wide downturn, but even then, this card will recover faster than anything else.

2. Charizard ex SAR — Paldean Fates (sv4pt5 #207)

High Confidence
Current: $190 AUD Target: $280-320 AUD

Charizard always wins long-term. This SAR is the chase card from a limited-print special set with excellent artwork. As Paldean Fates sealed product dries up, the singles will climb. The PSA 10 premium is already massive ($750+), which signals strong underlying demand.

3. JP Eevee Heroes Booster Box (Sealed)

High Confidence
Current: $320 AUD Target: $450-500 AUD

The Japanese equivalent of Evolving Skies. Out of print, no reprints coming, and the alt arts in this set are some of the best ever printed. Sealed Japanese product from popular sets appreciates consistently. Every box opened reduces supply. This is a hold-and-forget investment.

4. Rayquaza VMAX Alternate Art — Evolving Skies (#218)

High Confidence
Current: $340 AUD Target: $480-550 AUD

The second Evolving Skies alt art on this list, and for good reason. Rayquaza is a top-5 popular Pokemon, the artwork is breathtaking, and the same supply dynamics apply. Currently undervalued relative to Umbreon — the gap between the two should narrow over time.

Medium Confidence Picks

5. Pikachu ex SAR — 151 (#230)

Medium Confidence
Current: $95 AUD Target: $150-180 AUD

The 151 nostalgia factor is powerful and permanent. This Pikachu SAR is accessible at under $100, making it easy to accumulate. The 151 set was popular but not as heavily opened as main sets — supply is manageable. Pikachu is the mascot. The floor is solid.

6. Gengar VMAX Alternate Art — Fusion Strike (#271)

Medium Confidence
Current: $210 AUD Target: $300-350 AUD

Gengar riding through the city at night is one of the most recognisable modern card arts. Ghost-type collectors are devoted and this is their grail. Fusion Strike was a massive set with terrible pull rates, which actually helps scarcity. The card has been steadily climbing and I see no reason for it to stop.

7. Mew ex SAR — 151 (#232)

Medium Confidence
Current: $68 AUD Target: $110-130 AUD

The cheapest entry point on this list. At $68, the downside risk is minimal but the upside is real. Mew is universally loved, the 151 set has legs, and the artwork is clean. This is my top recommendation for collectors who want to start investing with a small budget.

8. Espeon V Alternate Art — Eevee Heroes (JP s6a #081)

Medium Confidence
Current: $155 AUD Target: $220-260 AUD

The most undervalued Eeveelution alt art from Eevee Heroes. While Umbreon and Sylveon get the headlines, Espeon has a dedicated fanbase and the JP quality means grading upside is real. As the Eevee Heroes set becomes harder to find, all the alt arts will appreciate — Espeon has the most room to grow.

9. Moonbreon — Paldean Fates (#131)

Medium Confidence
Current: $88 AUD Target: $130-160 AUD

The "budget Umbreon" that keeps getting more expensive. The Illustration Rare treatment of Umbreon under moonlight is stunning and it's become a collector favourite. At under $100 it's accessible, and the PSA 10 multiplier is one of the best in modern (5x from raw). Buy, grade the clean ones, hold the rest.

10. Base Set Charizard (Unlimited, LP-NM)

Medium Confidence
Current: $450-600 AUD Target: $600-800 AUD

The OG. Base Set Charizard is the Babe Ruth rookie card of Pokemon. Unlimited versions in decent condition have been relatively flat for a year, which means we're in an accumulation zone. The 30th anniversary of Pokemon is in 2026 — anniversary years always drive nostalgia purchasing. If you don't own one, now is the time.

Speculative Picks

11. Prismatic Evolutions Umbreon ex SAR

Speculative
Current: $280 AUD Target: $200 (dip) → $400+ (recovery)

This is a timing play. Prismatic Evolutions will get reprinted and prices will dip. When it does, that's your buying window. The long-term trajectory for the Umbreon SAR from this set is strongly upward, but the short-term could see a 20-30% correction. Patient money wins here. Wait for the dip, buy aggressively, hold for 12+ months.

12. JP Pokemon Card 151 Booster Box (Sealed)

Speculative
Current: $190 AUD Target: $280-320 AUD

151 has cross-generational appeal. The JP boxes are still relatively accessible at $190, which I think is undervalued given the set's long-term potential. Kanto nostalgia is the single most powerful force in the Pokemon market. These boxes will appreciate steadily over the next 2-3 years.

13. Sylveon VMAX Alternate Art — Evolving Skies (#212)

Speculative
Current: $200 AUD Target: $300-350 AUD

Living in Umbreon's shadow but quietly climbing. The Sylveon VMAX alt is the third most expensive card from Evolving Skies and has room to catch up. Sylveon's popularity has been increasing in the anime and competitive scene, and this is the definitive Sylveon card.

14. Lugia V Alternate Art — Silver Tempest (#186)

Speculative
Current: $175 AUD Target: $250-300 AUD

Silver Tempest doesn't get enough love. The Lugia V alt art with the lighthouse scene is arguably a top-10 card art in the entire Sword & Shield era. Lugia is an iconic legendary and Silver Tempest print runs were moderate. This is an under-the-radar pick that could surprise.

15. JP Shiny Charizard V — Shiny Star V (#307)

Speculative
Current: $120 AUD Target: $180-220 AUD

The black Charizard. This card is iconic in the JP market and has crossover appeal for English collectors. At $120 it's accessible, the artwork is unique (black shiny Charizard on black background), and it grades well due to Japanese quality control. A solid mid-range speculative play.

Portfolio Strategy

If I were building a Pokemon investment portfolio from scratch with $2,000 AUD:

  • 40% blue chips: Umbreon VMAX alt or Charizard ex SAR — proven performers
  • 30% sealed product: JP Eevee Heroes or 151 boxes — consistent appreciation
  • 20% mid-range singles: Moonbreon, Mew SAR, Pikachu SAR — growth potential with low downside
  • 10% speculative: One high-conviction speculative pick — bigger risk, bigger reward

Diversify across eras (vintage + modern), languages (EN + JP), and price points. Don't put everything into one card, no matter how confident you are. The market can humble anyone.

Review your positions quarterly, take profits when targets hit, and reinvest into the next opportunity. Card investing rewards patience and discipline more than anything else. Good luck out there. 📈
