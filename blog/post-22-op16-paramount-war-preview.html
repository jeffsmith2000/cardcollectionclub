One Piece OP16 Paramount War - Everything We Know

Published March 29, 2026 | By Card Collection Club

The One Piece Card Game keeps delivering, and OP16 Paramount War is shaping up to be one of the most anticipated English sets yet. Releasing June 12, 2026, this set takes us back to the iconic Marineford arc - arguably the most emotionally charged storyline in One Piece history. Here is what Australian collectors need to know.

The Marineford Factor

If you know One Piece, you know Marineford. The Summit War is the arc that broke the internet, made grown adults cry, and cemented One Piece as the goat of manga storytelling. Now those moments get translated into cards.

That means Whitebeard at his peak. Ace's final stand. Luffy's desperate rescue attempt. Admirals going all out. This is premium card art material, and Bandai knows it.

What to Expect

  • 24 packs per box, 12 cards per pack - Standard OP box configuration
  • New Leader cards with unique Impel Down and Marineford strategies
  • Characters spanning both Impel Down escape and Summit War battle
  • Manga Rare cards - The JP version confirmed 3 Manga Rares, a shift in rarity structure that could carry to EN

Chase Cards to Watch

While the full English card list is not confirmed, based on the Japanese OP-16 and the Marineford theme, these are the chase targets:

  • Whitebeard (Edward Newgate) Leader/SEC - The strongest man in the world at his peak. Any premium rarity version will be an instant grail.
  • Portgas D. Ace SEC/Manga Rare - Emotional significance plus collector demand. If Ace gets a Manga Rare, expect it to be the most valuable card in the set.
  • Monkey D. Luffy (Marineford) - Luffy in his desperate state, wearing Ivankov's outfit. Iconic moments = iconic cards.
  • Jinbe - The future Straw Hat's finest pre-crew moments happen in this arc. Strong collector appeal.
  • Admiral Trio - Akainu, Aokiji, Kizaru in full battle mode. Competitive and collector value.

AU Market Context

The One Piece TCG continues to grow in Australia. OP15 pre-release events this weekend (March 29-30) have been packed across the country. Treasure Cup events are expanding to more cities. The competitive scene is healthy and the collector market is thriving.

Current OP Sealed Market (AU)

  • OP01 Romance Dawn (Blue Bottom) - $7,600 - $9,500 AUD. Still climbing.
  • OP15 Booster Box (pre-order) - $130-150 AUD at most AU retailers
  • OP16 Booster Box (pre-order) - Not yet available, expect $130-160 AUD

Buying Strategy for OP16

Do This

  • Pre-order boxes as soon as AU retailers open allocations. Marineford hype will drive demand.
  • Hold 1-2 sealed boxes minimum. Marineford-themed sets have long-term appreciation potential (look at OP01).
  • Target Manga Rare and SEC cards for grading. The rarity shift makes these even more scarce.

Avoid This

  • Paying scalper prices for boxes. Supply has been steady for recent OP sets in AU.
  • Panic buying singles at release. Let the market settle for 2-3 weeks.
  • Sleeping on this set. Marineford nostalgia plus the manga rare addition makes OP16 a long-term winner.

Key Dates

  • June 12, 2026 - OP16 Paramount War English release
  • May-June 2026 - Treasure Cup events across AU (Brisbane May 10, Hurstville May 24, Perth June 7)
  • August 28, 2026 - OP17 English release (details TBA)

Paramount War is going to be special. The source material is peak One Piece, Bandai's card art quality keeps improving, and the AU market is hungry for it. Start planning your budget now.
