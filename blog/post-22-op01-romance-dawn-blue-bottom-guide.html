OP01 Romance Dawn Blue Bottom — The One Piece 1st Edition Explained

March 25, 2026 · 7 min read · Card Collection Club AU

If you've been in the One Piece TCG community for more than a few months, you've heard about the Blue Bottom. It's the most talked-about sealed product in the game's short history — and for good reason. This article breaks down exactly what the Blue Bottom is, why it commands a premium, and what the current market looks like in Australia.

🔵 Quick answer: The OP01 Blue Bottom is the first print run of Romance Dawn, identifiable by the blue strip at the bottom of the box. It contains pre-errata cards and is considered the true "1st edition" of One Piece TCG. Current AU market: $9,000–$12,000+ AUD for sealed boxes.

What Is the Blue Bottom?

When Bandai released OP-01 Romance Dawn in 2022 (the very first One Piece Card Game set), the initial print run had a distinctive blue strip along the bottom of the booster box. Subsequent print runs replaced this with a white strip — giving collectors a simple visual way to identify first print versus later prints.

But it's not just aesthetics. The Blue Bottom print also contains pre-errata versions of certain cards — cards that were later corrected or modified by Bandai. For competitive players and dedicated collectors, these original printings have historical significance that later white-bottom reprints simply don't have.

Think of it like this: the Blue Bottom is to One Piece TCG what the 1st Edition shadowless print is to Pokemon's Base Set. It's the origin. The beginning. And there are no more being printed.

Why Collectors Are Paying A Premium

Three things drive the Blue Bottom's value:

  • Fixed supply: Bandai moved to new print runs quickly. The total number of Blue Bottom boxes is finite and shrinking as collectors open them.
  • Pre-errata cards: Certain cards in the first print are different from later versions — a draw for completionists and historians of the game.
  • First set, first print: Romance Dawn is where One Piece TCG started. It contains some of the game's most iconic cards — Monkey D. Luffy, Roronoa Zoro, and the leaders that defined the meta. Having them in their original print matters.

The gap between Blue and White Bottom is enormous — and has been widening. The White Bottom is still a significant collector item (OP01 booster boxes in general have appreciated massively from original retail of $130–145 AUD). But the Blue Bottom is in a different league entirely.

How to Identify a Blue Bottom

It's straightforward once you know what to look for:

  • Look at the bottom edge of the booster box
  • First print = blue/dark strip along the bottom edge
  • Later prints = white strip
  • Also check: the Japanese text on first prints uses slightly different printing compared to reprints

⚠️ Fakes exist. As prices have climbed, counterfeit Blue Bottom boxes have appeared. Only buy from reputable sellers with clear photos, ideally with graded or verified purchase history. Never buy based on photos alone for any box over $1,000.

Should You Buy One?

If you're asking whether a Blue Bottom box is a good investment at current prices — it depends entirely on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

Bull case: One Piece is still growing globally. The anime is in its final arc. When it concludes, collector interest typically spikes. Blue Bottom boxes are the closest thing One Piece TCG has to a 1st Edition Base Set Pokemon box. In 5 years, $12,000 could look cheap.

Bear case: At these prices, liquidity is thin. Finding a buyer for a $10K box takes time. The One Piece TCG is only 3–4 years old — if the game loses momentum, high-end sealed could stall. And you'd need to store it properly (temperature controlled, out of sunlight) to maintain condition.

✅ CCC Take: If you already own a Blue Bottom (like many serious AU collectors do), do not open it. Do not sell it unless you need the liquidity. These will be worth more in 3–5 years than they are today, assuming the One Piece TCG continues its current growth trajectory. If you're considering buying one — be patient, verify authenticity rigorously, and only pay with buyer protection.
