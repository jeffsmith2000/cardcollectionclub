OP15: Adventure on Kami's Island — What to Buy & What to Chase

March 26, 2026 · 7 min read · Card Collection Club AU

One Piece TCG continues its relentless expansion with OP15: Adventure on Kami's Island, dropping April 3, 2026. This set covers the Skypiea arc, one of the most iconic storylines in One Piece, and marks a significant milestone: Bandai's push toward unified worldwide releases. Here's your complete guide to what's coming, what's worth chasing, and how to position yourself in the AU market.

What's in OP15?

Adventure on Kami's Island dives deep into the Skypiea saga, featuring Enel, Wyper, the Shandians, and of course the Straw Hat crew's sky island adventures. Key details:

  • Arc focus: Skypiea / Upper Yard / Kami's Island
  • New Leaders: Enel, Wyper, and potentially Gan Fall
  • Expected card count: 120+ (standard booster set)
  • Worldwide release: Part of Bandai's 2026 unified schedule
  • Booster box RRP: ~$125-140 AUD (24 packs)

Chase Cards to Watch

Enel (SEC / Manga Art)

Enel is one of the most popular villains in One Piece history. Any SEC or Special Art version of Enel will be the top chase card of this set. JP prices for Enel SEC cards have historically hit $200-400+ USD. Expect similar demand in English.

Luffy (Skypiea) — Alt Art

Straw Hat Luffy in his Skypiea outfit punching out Enel. This is the kind of iconic manga moment that drives collector demand. If this card exists in the set (and it almost certainly does), it'll be a top-3 pull.

Wyper (Leader / SR)

The Shandian warrior doesn't have many cards in the OPTCG yet. A new Leader card or SR could be a sleeper pick, especially if Wyper sees competitive play in the meta.

Nami (Skypiea) — Illustration

Nami cards consistently command premium prices across all One Piece sets. A Skypiea-themed Nami illustration card would be an instant collector favourite.

Sealed Product Strategy

Booster Boxes

Buy rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

At ~$125-140 AUD, OP15 booster boxes are a strong pickup. One Piece sealed product has a proven track record of appreciation. OP01 boxes are now $1,300-1,800+ AUD. While OP15 won't reach those heights (OP01 had first-set scarcity), buying at retail and holding 12+ months is a reliable play.

Prerelease Events

Check your local game stores for OP15 prerelease events in late March. Prerelease promos can carry decent value, and it's a great way to see the set early while supporting your LGS.

💡 AU Market Insight: One Piece TCG has been gaining ground in Australia throughout 2025-2026. Convention vendors report increasing demand for OP sealed product, and the unified worldwide release schedule means AU collectors no longer have to wait months after JP. This is bullish for OP15 first-wave pricing.

How OP15 Fits the 2026 Roadmap

Bandai has an aggressive release schedule for 2026:

  • OP14: The Azure Sea's Seven (released Jan 16) — Wano focus
  • OP15: Adventure on Kami's Island (April 3) — Skypiea focus
  • OP16: Expected ~June 2026 — arc TBC
  • OP17: 4th Anniversary set — Expected August 2026

The 4th Anniversary set (OP17) is likely to be the biggest One Piece release of 2026, similar to how anniversary sets in Pokemon command premium pricing. If you have limited budget, consider splitting between OP15 and saving for OP17.

The Bottom Line

OP15 is a must-buy for One Piece collectors. Skypiea is a beloved arc, Enel is a fan-favourite villain, and the unified worldwide release gives AU collectors equal footing. Buy 1-2 booster boxes at retail, hold one sealed, open one for fun. Chase the Enel SEC as a single if you can find it under $150 AUD in the first two weeks.
