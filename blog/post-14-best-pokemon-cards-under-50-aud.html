Best Pokémon Cards Under $50 AUD

By Ian N · February 18, 2026 · 8 min read

Not every great Pokémon card needs to cost a fortune. Some of the most beautiful, iconic, and even investment-worthy cards in the modern TCG can be picked up for under $50 AUD — if you know where to look. Whether you're building a binder, starting a collection, or hunting for cards with upside potential, this list has something for everyone.

All prices listed are based on current Australian market values for near-mint raw copies as of February 2026.

1. Charizard ex (Obsidian Flames #215 — Art Rare)

Yes, you can own a Charizard art rare for under fifty bucks. The Obsidian Flames Charizard ex features stunning artwork with the fire lizard perched atop a volcanic ridge. It's a proper display piece and carries the Charizard name premium without the Charizard price tag.

~$40–$48 AUD

2. Mew ex (Pokémon 151 #151 — Full Art)

Mew is number 151 in a set called 151 — you can't script it better than that. This full art features a clean, minimalist design with Mew floating serenely against a cosmic background. The 151 set continues to hold strong, and this card sits right at the sweet spot for value.

~$35–$45 AUD

3. Iono (Paldea Evolved #269 — Special Art Rare)

The Iono SAR from Paldea Evolved has dipped from its initial highs, making now an excellent time to grab one. The artwork shows Iono mid-stream with her Bellibolt, capturing the electric personality that made her an instant fan favourite. Trainer SARs tend to have incredible long-term legs.

~$42–$50 AUD

4. Umbreon ex (Obsidian Flames #227 — Special Art Rare)

Umbreon cards always command attention, and this SAR from Obsidian Flames is no exception. The nighttime cityscape backdrop with Umbreon's glowing rings is atmospheric and gorgeous. Currently sitting just under the $50 mark, this is one to grab before the inevitable Eeveelution tax kicks in.

~$44–$50 AUD

5. Pikachu (Pokémon 151 #172 — Illustration Rare)

This adorable Illustration Rare shows Pikachu surrounded by a garden of flowers, illustrated in a watercolour style that's become signature for the modern TCG. It's pure joy in card form. At under $30 AUD, it's an absolute steal.

~$22–$28 AUD

6. Gardevoir ex (Scarlet & Violet #245 — Special Art Rare)

Gardevoir's SAR from the base Scarlet & Violet set features an elegant ballroom scene that could hang in a gallery. The card's beauty speaks for itself, and at current prices it's wildly undervalued compared to other trainer-adjacent SARs.

~$28–$35 AUD

7. Arcanine ex (Pokémon 151 #167 — Illustration Rare)

The legendary Pokémon gets a beautiful Illustration Rare treatment in 151, with Arcanine bounding through an open field. Gen 1 nostalgia combined with excellent artwork makes this a budget binder essential.

~$15–$20 AUD

8. Miraidon ex (Scarlet & Violet #253 — Special Art Rare)

The box legendary of Pokémon Violet gets a dynamic SAR showing it racing through a futuristic cityscape. Miraidon is the face of the generation, and at current prices under $40 AUD, it feels significantly underpriced for a box art legendary SAR.

~$32–$38 AUD

9. Eevee (Pokémon 151 #175 — Illustration Rare)

Eevee surrounded by all its evolutions in silhouette? Absolute perfection. This Illustration Rare from 151 tugs at the heartstrings and represents the eternal appeal of the Eevee family. A genuine bargain at current prices.

~$18–$25 AUD

10. Mewtwo ex (Pokémon 151 #183 — Art Rare)

The OG psychic powerhouse. Mewtwo's Art Rare from 151 shows it hovering menacingly in its laboratory setting — a direct callback to the first Pokémon movie. Mewtwo is one of those Pokémon whose cards never truly go out of style, and at under $30 AUD, this is a must-have.

~$25–$30 AUD

Honourable Mentions

  • Gengar ex (Obsidian Flames #263, SAR) — ~$38 AUD. Ghost type fans, this one's for you.
  • Alakazam ex (Pokémon 151 #181, Art Rare) — ~$15 AUD. Criminally undervalued Gen 1 art.
  • Snorlax (Pokémon 151 #179, Illustration Rare) — ~$20 AUD. Snorlax sleeping in a field. Need we say more?
  • Koraidon ex (Scarlet & Violet #254, SAR) — ~$30 AUD. The Scarlet legendary deserves more love.

Final Thoughts

You don't need deep pockets to build a stunning Pokémon collection. The Scarlet & Violet era has delivered some of the most beautiful cards in the TCG's history, and many of them remain remarkably affordable. The cards on this list represent a mix of investment potential, collector appeal, and pure aesthetic beauty — all for under $50 AUD each.

Start grabbing these while they're still cheap. Future you will be glad you did. Happy collecting! 🎴
