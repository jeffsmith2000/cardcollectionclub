Top 10 Pokemon Cards Worth Grading in 2026 — PSA ROI Analysis

I
Ian Nguyen
February 17, 2026 · 12 min read

Let's be real — not every card is worth grading. At $50-150 USD per card (roughly $75-230 AUD depending on service level), PSA grading is a serious investment. I've sent off hundreds of cards over the past three years and learned the hard way that grading a $20 card "just because" is a great way to light money on fire.

This guide breaks down 10 cards where the maths actually works. I'm talking real profit margins based on current February 2026 market prices in AUD, factoring in PSA submission costs, shipping, and insurance. These are cards where the spread between raw and graded is wide enough to justify the expense — even at Economy service ($75 AUD).

All prices are approximate AUD based on recent eBay AU sold listings and TCGPlayer conversions as of mid-February 2026. PSA grading cost is calculated at Economy tier (~$75 AUD including return shipping from Australia).

1. Charizard ex Special Art Rare — Paldean Fates (sv4pt5 #207)

The undisputed king of modern grading ROI. This card sits at around $180 raw, which is already a solid card. But a PSA 10 pushes it to $750+. The centering on these is notoriously inconsistent — Paldean Fates had some rough quality control — so a confirmed PSA 10 carries serious premium. Pop counts are still relatively low for 10s compared to the overall print run.

Grading tip: Check left-right centering first. The gold border makes even slight miscuts painfully obvious. If it's 55/45 or worse, don't bother.

2. Umbreon VMAX Alternate Art — Evolving Skies (#215)

This card needs no introduction. It's been the flagship modern chase card since 2021 and shows zero signs of slowing down. At $550 raw you're already deep, but PSA 10 copies consistently clear $2,200 AUD. The $1,575 profit margin is the fattest on this list.

The catch? Pull rates were brutal, and finding a mint copy in the wild is tough. Most raw copies floating around have whitening or print lines. If you've got a clean one from a freshly opened pack, send it immediately.

3. Pikachu VMAX Rainbow — Vivid Voltage (#188)

Chunky Pikachu! This chonky boy has meme status that drives real demand. The rainbow rare treatment on a round Pikachu VMAX is genuinely iconic. Raw copies around $280, PSA 10 hitting $1,100. The surface on these scratches easily, so pack-fresh is critical.

Grading tip: Use a jeweller's loupe to check the front surface. Rainbow rares show micro-scratches under light that you won't see with the naked eye.

4. Moonbreon — Paldean Fates (#131)

The "budget Umbreon" that punches way above its weight. At $85 raw it's the most accessible card on this list, but PSA 10s are fetching $420. That's a 5x multiplier from raw to gem mint. The illustration rare treatment with the moonlit background is gorgeous and appeals to both collectors and Umbreon fanatics.

5. Charizard UPC Promo — Crown Zenith (GG70)

The Ultra Premium Collection promo Charizard has become a modern staple. At $95 raw it's very grading-friendly from a cost perspective. PSA 10s hitting $480 means you're looking at $310 profit. The metal-style card is relatively resilient to surface damage but watch for edge whitening from the packaging.

6. Rayquaza VMAX Alternate Art — Evolving Skies (#218)

The second Evolving Skies monster on this list. Rayquaza flying through the clouds is one of the most beautiful arts in the modern era. $320 raw, $1,400 at PSA 10. Dragon-type nostalgia plus stunning artwork equals sustained demand.

These tend to grade well compared to Umbreon — the darker colour palette hides minor imperfections. Still check that centering carefully though.

7. Mew ex Special Art Rare — 151 (#232)

The 151 set hit different. This Mew SAR with the psychic energy swirl is a legitimate piece of art. At only $65 raw, the barrier to entry is low. PSA 10 copies at $350 give you a clean $210 profit. The 151 nostalgia factor isn't going anywhere — Kanto sells forever.

8. Espeon V Alternate Art — Eevee Heroes (JP s6a #081)

Japanese cards grade beautifully. Espeon V Alt from Eevee Heroes has that premium JP quality control working in your favour. $150 raw, $700 at PSA 10. The Eeveelution tax is real and this is one of the more undervalued pieces from the set.

Pro tip: JP cards generally have better centering and surface quality. Your odds of getting a 10 are meaningfully higher than English equivalents.

9. Gengar VMAX Alternate Art — Fusion Strike (#271)

Gengar riding through the city at night. This is the card that made Fusion Strike worth opening despite the massive set size. $200 raw, $850 for PSA 10. Ghost-type collectors are passionate and this is the undisputed best Gengar card in the modern era.

10. Prismatic Evolutions Umbreon ex SAR

The new hotness. Prismatic Evolutions dropped in January 2026 and this Umbreon ex SAR is already commanding serious premiums. Raw copies are sitting around $250 with PSA 10s estimated at $1,200+ based on early submissions. The hype is real — this set is Evolving Skies 2.0 in terms of collector frenzy.

Warning: population counts are extremely low right now, so PSA 10 prices could come down as more copies get graded. But they could also go up if gem mint rates are low. It's a calculated risk, but the spread is massive.

When NOT to Grade

A few quick rules I live by:

  • If the raw card is under $50 AUD — the maths rarely works at Economy tier unless the PSA 10 multiplier is 6x+
  • If you can see any whitening, dents, or scratches — a PSA 8 or lower often sells for barely more than raw
  • If pop counts are already flooded — some modern cards have 5,000+ PSA 10s, which crushes the premium
  • If you're grading for "insurance" — only makes sense for cards worth $200+ raw

Submission Tips for Aussie Collectors

Submitting from Australia adds complexity. Here's what I recommend:

  • Use a local middleman like Graded Gem or Lux Cards — they bulk ship to PSA and pass on volume discounts
  • Budget $75-100 AUD total per card at Economy tier (grading fee + domestic shipping to middleman + return shipping)
  • Current turnaround for Economy is roughly 4-6 months — factor that into your ROI calculation
  • For cards worth $500+ raw, consider Express ($250 AUD) for the 10-day turnaround

The grading game is all about discipline. Grade the right cards at the right time and you can easily double or triple your money. Grade everything and you'll bleed fees. Pick your spots wisely.
