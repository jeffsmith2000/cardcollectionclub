Convention Tips for Buyers

💡 Get there early. The best deals — slabs, underpriced bulk lots, rare singles — go in the first hour. Most vendors would rather deal on day one than pack unsold stock.

  • Bring cash. Card readers fail, EFTPOS goes down, and some vendors only take cash. $500 cash is the convention minimum.
  • Know your prices beforehand. Check eBay sold listings before you go. Vendors will respect you more and negotiate faster if you can quote recent sales.
  • Bring sleeves and top loaders. Any singles you buy need protection immediately. Don't rely on vendors having spare supplies.
  • Walk the whole floor before buying. The first table isn't always the best price. Do a lap first.

Convention Tips for Sellers

  • Price to sell. People at conventions want deals. If you want book value, use eBay. At conventions, price 10–20% under market to move stock.
  • Organise your display. Binders for bulk, top loaders for premium singles, labelled boxes for sealed. Tidy displays get more attention and trust.
  • Accept cash AND card. Get a Square or Tyro reader. You will lose sales without card payment.
  • Bundle slow stock. "$50 for any 10 cards in this binder" clears stock faster than individual pricing.
