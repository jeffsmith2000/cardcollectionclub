Surging Stars Set Preview + Investment Picks

By John C · February 18, 2026 · 9 min read

The Pokémon TCG Surging Stars set is shaping up to be one of the most anticipated releases of 2026, and we've been tracking every reveal, leak, and rumour to bring you the definitive preview. Whether you're a collector hunting chase cards, a player looking for competitive staples, or an investor planning your next moves, this guide has you covered.

What We Know So Far

Surging Stars is expected to be a late Scarlet & Violet era set, potentially one of the final mainline expansions before the generation transition. Japanese reveals have given us a strong picture of the set's direction, and it's looking packed with both collector appeal and competitive relevance.

Set Details (Preliminary):
Expected card count: 190+ (main set) with 60+ secret rares
Themes: Stellar Tera types, cosmic aesthetics, fan-favourite Pokémon returns
Expected English release: Mid-2026
Products: Booster boxes, ETBs, booster bundles, special collections

Confirmed and Rumoured Chase Cards

Rayquaza ex (Stellar Tera — Special Art Rare)

Rayquaza is back, and this time it's getting the Stellar Tera treatment with a cosmic-themed Special Art Rare that early leaks suggest will rival the legendary Evolving Skies Rayquaza VMAX Alt Art. Rayquaza cards are historically among the best performers in the hobby — the Evolving Skies alt art went from $80 to $400+ AUD. If the artwork delivers, this will be THE chase card of 2026.

Predicted launch price: $150–$250 AUD

Mewtwo ex (Stellar Tera — Special Art Rare)

Mewtwo in Stellar form is a match made in collector heaven. Early artwork descriptions point to a psychic-cosmic composition with Mewtwo surrounded by swirling stellar energy. Given Mewtwo's enduring popularity and the Stellar Tera aesthetic, this should be a strong performer.

Predicted launch price: $100–$180 AUD

Pikachu ex (Stellar Tera — Special Art Rare)

Every major set needs its Pikachu chase card, and Surging Stars appears to deliver. The Stellar Tera Pikachu is rumoured to feature a dazzling night-sky backdrop with Pikachu conducting lightning across a star-filled canvas. Pikachu SARs have a floor of $80–$100 AUD even in less popular sets, so this should hold well.

Predicted launch price: $80–$130 AUD

Gardevoir ex (Stellar Tera — Illustration Rare)

Gardevoir continues to be a reliable chase card across every set it appears in. The Stellar Tera version is expected to showcase Gardevoir in an ethereal, galaxy-inspired setting. Gardevoir IRs tend to sit in the $30–$60 AUD range, making this an accessible collector piece.

Predicted launch price: $35–$60 AUD

N's Resolve (Supporter — Special Art Rare)

If confirmed, a Supporter SAR featuring N would be enormous. N is one of the most popular Pokémon characters ever created, and his cards command massive premiums. Early rumours suggest a dramatic artwork showing N with Reshiram and Zekrom in the background. If this card exists, it could rival Erika's Invitation for the most valuable modern trainer card.

Predicted launch price: $120–$200 AUD

Competitive Cards to Watch

Beyond the collector chase cards, Surging Stars is expected to introduce several competitively relevant cards:

  • Stellar Tera support cards: New trainer and energy cards that specifically boost Stellar Tera Pokémon could reshape the competitive meta.
  • Rayquaza ex: If the competitive version is playable, expect demand from both collectors and players — a powerful combination for price support.
  • Draw/search supporters: New supporter cards with competitive utility tend to hold value due to tournament demand across multiple decks.
  • Stadium cards: Powerful stadium cards can become meta staples overnight, and early hints suggest Surging Stars includes at least one format-defining stadium.

Investment Strategy: Pre-Release

What to Buy Now

  • Pre-order booster boxes at retail (~$170–$185 AUD): Lock in current retail prices. If the set is popular, secondary market prices will jump on release day.
  • Pre-order ETBs ($70–$85 AUD): ETBs are the most liquid sealed product and have the best long-term track record for appreciation.
  • Japanese product (when available): Japanese Surging Stars equivalent boxes will be cheaper and potentially harder to find later.

What to Wait On

  • Singles: Always wait 2–4 weeks after release for singles prices to settle. The initial hype premium can be 30–50% above the eventual floor price.
  • Graded submissions: Wait at least 3 months before grading Surging Stars cards. Let the market establish which cards have genuine staying power.

Investment Strategy: Post-Release

Week 1–2: Observe

Track opening results, pull rate data, and initial market prices. Don't buy singles yet unless you spot a genuine undervaluation.

Week 3–4: Start Buying Singles

Most cards hit their initial price floor around week 3–4. This is your window to acquire chase cards at reasonable prices. Target the top 3–5 SARs and any competitively relevant cards.

Month 2–3: Sealed Accumulation

If the set proves popular, start accumulating additional sealed product. Booster boxes and ETBs at or near retail are the safest play. The goal is to build a position before the print run ends.

Month 6+: Grading Window

Six months post-release, you'll have clear data on which cards are winners. Submit your best raw copies for grading. Focus on cards that have maintained or grown in value — they're the ones the market has validated.

How This Set Compares

Surging Stars has the potential to be one of the defining sets of the Scarlet & Violet era. The combination of:

  • Beloved Pokémon (Rayquaza, Mewtwo, Pikachu)
  • Popular mechanics (Stellar Tera)
  • Cosmic aesthetics that photograph brilliantly
  • Late-era positioning (potential scarcity as SV winds down)

...gives it a profile similar to Evolving Skies in the Sword & Shield era. We're not saying it'll match Evolving Skies — that's a high bar — but the fundamentals are strong.

Risks to Consider

  • Overhype: If expectations get too high, the initial price spike could be severe, leading to a sharper correction.
  • Print run uncertainty: The Pokémon Company has been adjusting print runs. A larger-than-expected print could suppress sealed values.
  • Generation transition: If the next generation launches shortly after, collector attention may shift quickly.
  • Competition: Other sets releasing around the same time (including One Piece TCG) could split collector budgets.

Our Bottom Line

Surging Stars looks like the real deal. The Pokémon lineup is stacked, the Stellar Tera aesthetic is gorgeous, and the late-SV-era timing creates natural scarcity potential. We're planning to allocate a meaningful portion of our 2026 investment budget to this set — both sealed product and select singles.

Pre-order your booster boxes now, wait patiently on singles, and keep your eyes on the Japanese reveals for early intelligence. This is the kind of set that rewards preparation.

Stars are surging, legends. Get ready. ⭐
