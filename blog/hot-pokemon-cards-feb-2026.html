Prismatic Evolutions — The Hype is Real

Let's start with the elephant in the room. Prismatic Evolutions has been the most hyped Pokemon set since Evolving Skies, and the market is reflecting it. The Umbreon ex SAR has jumped 40% in the past month alone, climbing from $200 to $280 AUD raw. This card is drawing direct comparisons to the Moonbreon and the OG Evolving Skies Umbreon VMAX alt.

What's driving it? Supply constraints. Prismatic Evolutions ETBs are selling for $80-90 AUD above RRP on the secondary market because Pokemon Company didn't print enough for the demand. When product is hard to open, the singles get expensive. Simple economics.

The Sylveon ex SAR is the quiet mover here — up 36% and getting attention from the Eeveelution collector crowd who missed the Evolving Skies boat. At $150 it's still accessible, which is part of the appeal.

My take: Prismatic Evolutions will get a reprint wave. When it does, singles will dip 15-25%. If you're buying to flip, be careful with timing. If you're buying to keep, the long-term trajectory is still up.

Evolving Skies — The Run Continues

Evolving Skies alt arts just keep climbing. The Umbreon VMAX alt hit $580 AUD this month, up from $520. The Rayquaza VMAX alt is at $340. Sealed Evolving Skies booster boxes are sitting at $650+ and showing no signs of coming down.

Why is a 2021 set still climbing in 2026? Because it's out of print and the supply is genuinely finite. Every sealed box opened reduces the remaining supply. Every graded copy that gets slabbed reduces the raw supply. The Evolving Skies flywheel is real — as prices go up, fewer people want to sell, which makes prices go up more.

If you've been holding Evolving Skies singles, congratulations. If you're thinking about getting in, the honest truth is that $580 for the Umbreon feels expensive but people said the same thing at $300 and $400.

Charizard Watch

Charizard cards are having a mixed month. The Paldean Fates Charizard ex SAR is up to $190 (+12%), riding the general modern market momentum. But the Obsidian Flames Charizard ex (#223) has dipped slightly to $85 (-6%). That tera-type Charizard was everywhere — massive print run, tons of product opened — and the market is reflecting the oversupply.

The takeaway? Not all Charizards are created equal. Scarcity matters more than the character on the card. The Paldean Fates Charizard SAR has the scarcity factor (special art rare from a limited subset), while the Obsidian Flames version was a standard chase card from a heavily printed main set.

Japanese Cards Trending

JP cards are having a moment in the Australian market. Here's what I'm seeing:

  • Eevee Heroes booster boxes — $320 AUD and climbing. This set is the Japanese equivalent of Evolving Skies and the overlap in collector interest is massive.
  • VSTAR Universe boxes — $180 AUD, up from $150 last month. The god pack mechanic gives these boxes a gambling appeal that keeps demand high.
  • Shiny Treasure ex boxes — $130 AUD, relatively stable. Good value compared to the EN equivalent (Paldean Fates).
  • JP exclusive promos — Lottery promos, Centre exclusive cards, and tournament prizes are getting scooped up by Australian collectors who recognise the value of Japan-only products.

The AUD/JPY exchange rate is relatively favourable right now (~95 yen to the dollar), making Japanese imports more attractive. If you're not already looking at JP cards, you're missing opportunities.

What's Cooling Off

Not everything is up. A few notable declines:

  • Giratina VSTAR (Lost Origin) — Down 13% to $48. The competitive demand that was propping up this card has faded as the standard format rotates.
  • Crown Zenith Galarian Gallery cards — Most non-Charizard cards from this set have plateaued or dipped slightly. The novelty has worn off.
  • Paldea Evolved bulk rares — Ting-Lu ex, Wo-Chien ex, and other competitive-only cards are cratering as they rotate out of standard.
  • Modern ETBs at retail — Scarlet & Violet base, Paldea Evolved, and Obsidian Flames ETBs can be found at or below RRP now. The "everything Pokemon is sold out" era is over for non-hyped sets.

Sealed Product Watch

Quick snapshot of sealed product trends:

  • Evolving Skies Booster Box: $650+ AUD (up from $550 six months ago)
  • Prismatic Evolutions ETB: $120-130 AUD (RRP $75 — 60-70% premium)
  • 151 Booster Bundle: $85-95 AUD (stable, slight premium over RRP)
  • Paldean Fates ETB: $75-80 AUD (near RRP, great value)
  • Crown Zenith ETB: $90 AUD (slight premium, good long-term hold)

February Picks

Here's where I'm putting my money this month:

  • Buy: Paldean Fates ETBs at near-RRP — this set ages well and the Charizard SAR keeps it relevant
  • Buy: JP Eevee Heroes singles if you can find them at reasonable prices
  • Hold: Evolving Skies anything — the trajectory is clear
  • Sell: Prismatic Evolutions if you pulled well — take profits before the reprint hits
  • Avoid: Obsidian Flames singles — supply is too high, demand is flat
