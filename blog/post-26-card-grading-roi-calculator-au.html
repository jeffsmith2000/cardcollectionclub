Card Grading ROI: When Is It Actually Worth Grading in Australia?

March 26, 2026 · 10 min read · Card Collection Club AU

Every collector eventually asks the question: should I grade this card? The answer isn't always yes. In fact, for most cards, grading is a net loss. This guide breaks down the real costs for Australian collectors, gives you a simple ROI framework, and shows exactly when grading makes financial sense.

The True Cost of Grading from Australia

Most grading cost calculators are US-focused and ignore the reality of AU shipping, insurance, and currency conversion. Here's what it actually costs in 2026:

Bulk submissions through a middleman service can bring this down to $80-120 per card, but there's usually a longer wait time (3-6 months).

The ROI Formula

Simple math that most collectors skip:

Grading ROI = (Graded Value - Raw Value - Total Grading Cost) / Total Grading Cost x 100

For grading to be profitable, the graded card needs to sell for more than the raw card PLUS all grading costs. Sounds obvious, but you'd be surprised how many people grade $20 cards and lose money.

When Grading IS Worth It

✅ Example: Psyduck Munch (Raw NM ~$400 AUD)

Raw value: ~$400 AUD

PSA 9 value: ~$1,500-2,300 AUD

Grading cost: ~$180 AUD (standard PSA + shipping)

Profit if PSA 9: $920-1,720 AUD (159-297% ROI)

Even if it comes back PSA 8 (~$800 AUD), you're still up $220. Clear grade.

✅ Example: Alt Art Luffy (OP01, Raw NM ~$300 AUD)

Raw value: ~$300 AUD

PSA 10 value: ~$800-1,200 AUD

PSA 9 value: ~$400-500 AUD

Grading cost: ~$180 AUD

Profit if PSA 10: $320-720 AUD. Break-even if PSA 9.

Worth grading only if the card is in excellent condition and you're confident in a 10.

When Grading is NOT Worth It

❌ Example: Random modern holo ($15 AUD raw)

Raw value: ~$15 AUD

PSA 10 value: ~$40-60 AUD

Grading cost: ~$150 AUD

Loss even at PSA 10: -$90 to -$110 AUD

This is the most common mistake. Modern holos almost never justify grading costs from Australia.

❌ Example: Common V/VSTAR card ($5-10 AUD raw)

Even a PSA 10 on most V/VSTAR cards won't crack $50 AUD. You'd need to grade 3-4 cards just to break even on one. Don't do it.

The Minimum Raw Value Rule

Here's a rule of thumb that will save you money:

Don't grade any card with a raw value under $100 AUD unless it's vintage (pre-2005) or has exceptional PSA 10 multiplier (5x+ raw value).

For AU collectors specifically, the shipping overhead means the breakeven threshold is higher than for US-based collectors. A US collector might profitably grade a $50 raw card. You probably can't.

Cost-Saving Tips for AU Graders

  1. Use a middleman/group submission service. Companies like Cardboard Kaijus, PSA Australia Group Subs, and local LGS group subs can cut your per-card cost by 30-40%.
  2. Wait for PSA AU events. PSA occasionally does walk-in events in Sydney and Melbourne where you can submit in person, saving return shipping costs.
  3. Consider CGC for budget submissions. CGC is cheaper than PSA and gaining acceptance, especially for modern cards.
  4. Batch your submissions. Shipping 10 cards costs roughly the same as shipping 1. Bulk up before you submit.
  5. Grade at conventions. Some AU conventions (Cardcon, Trading Card Summit) have on-site grading submission booths with discounted rates.

PSA vs CGC vs BGS: Which One for AU Collectors?

  • PSA: Highest resale premium, longest wait times, most expensive. Best for high-value cards ($200+ raw) where the PSA label adds the most value.
  • CGC: Best value for money, growing in acceptance, sub-grades included. Great for mid-range cards ($80-200 raw) and modern sets.
  • BGS: The premium option for vintage and the "Black Label" chase. Only worth it for potentially perfect vintage cards where BGS 10 commands a massive premium.

The Bottom Line

Grading from Australia is expensive. The shipping and currency conversion add $60-100 AUD per card compared to US-based collectors. That means you need to be more selective about what you grade.

Quick checklist before grading:

  1. Is the raw card worth $100+ AUD? If no, stop.
  2. Does a PSA 9/10 sell for 2x+ the raw value? If no, stop.
  3. Is the card in genuinely excellent condition (clean centering, no whitening, no surface scratches)? If no, stop.
  4. Can you batch it with other cards to reduce shipping costs? Always preferred.

Grade smart, not often. Your wallet will thank you.
