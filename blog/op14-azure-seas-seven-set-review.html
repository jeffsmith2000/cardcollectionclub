OP-14 Azure Sea's Seven: Set Review and Top Cards

By Card Collection Club | March 2026 | Set Review

OP-14 Azure Sea's Seven continues the Seraphim arc narrative with a focus on the Seven Warlords reimagined as Pacifista-adjacent clones. The set introduces new Seraphim leaders and significantly boosts Warlords-themed decks. For collectors, it's a set with exceptional alt-art pulls and a few strong investment targets. Here's our full breakdown.

📌 Set at a glance: OP-14 releases Q1 2026 (EN). Booster box (24 packs): ~$155 AUD RRP. Pull rates: 1 SR per box average, 1 Secret Rare per 3–4 boxes. Features 121 cards including Parallel Rares and Alternate Arts.

Overall Set Rating

★★★★☆ — Strong collector set, moderate investment potential

OP-14 is primarily a collector's set rather than a competitive overhaul. The Seraphim theme has massive narrative appeal — some of the most visually striking card art of any One Piece set. Investment potential is moderate near launch but several specific cards have strong long-term prospects tied to character popularity.

Top Cards to Watch

CHASE CARD

S-Hawk (Seraphim) — Parallel Secret Rare

The Mihawk Seraphim parallel art is visually stunning — dark blue ocean tones with the green eye glowing effect. Hawk is one of the most popular characters in the One Piece universe, and the Seraphim version carries all that character equity. Expected AU price at set release: $200–$350 raw. PSA 10 outlook: $500–$800 within 12 months if character popularity sustains. The chase card of the set.

VALUE BUY

S-Snake (Seraphim Boa Hancock) — SR

Boa Hancock's seraphim card benefits from the original Hancock's extreme popularity. Any card connected to Hancock tends to hold value well. The regular SR should be attainable at $60–$90 AU raw near release, with potential to move to $120–$180 within 6 months as box openings slow. Better value play than the parallel for those working with a tighter budget.

SLEEPER

S-Bear (Seraphim Bartholomew Kuma) — Leader SR

Kuma's Seraphim version is getting a leader card that enables a new Seraphim-focused deck archetype. While it may not top the competitive meta immediately, leader SRs from sets that introduce new archetypes historically appreciate as the archetype develops. AU price target at release: $45–$70. Long-term hold candidate if Seraphim decks become a tournament staple.

AVOID

Non-Seraphim Warlord SR Reprints

OP-14 includes a handful of updated Warlord cards (not Seraphim) that are essentially power creep replacements for older versions. These have lower collector appeal, aren't new characters, and will likely depress prices on the older versions. Buy only if you need them for competitive play at retail.

Collector Appeal: Alternate Arts

OP-14's alternate art cards are among the best in the One Piece TCG's history. The art direction leans heavily into underwater/deep sea aesthetics with dramatic lighting and colour work. The top 5 alt arts in collector desirability:

  1. S-Hawk (ocean background, glowing eye) — highest
  2. Doflamingo (web/strings motif) — very high
  3. S-Snake (stone garden aesthetic) — high
  4. Crocodile (desert storm, dark palette) — high
  5. Gecko Moria (shadow realm setting) — moderate-high

Should You Buy Boxes?

At $155 AUD RRP, OP-14 boxes offer solid value if you pull chase cards, but buying for investment purposes is premature at launch. Strategy recommendations:

  • Buy 1 box for personal pulls: The experience is solid, pull rates are fair
  • Target singles over boxes: For the top 5 cards listed above, buying singles directly is more cost-effective than chasing through boxes
  • Sealed investment: Not yet — wait 6+ months for print run data before committing sealed boxes as investments

AU Market Outlook (6–12 Month)

OP-14 is expected to sell consistently rather than explosively — the Seraphim theme has deep lore fans but isn't a mainstream arc peak like Marineford or Gear 5. We expect:

  • S-Hawk Parallel: $350–$600 PSA 10 by year end
  • S-Snake SR: Gradual appreciation to $100–$150 raw
  • Box prices: $170–$220 AU in 12 months as stock depletes
