Mega Evolution: Chaos Rising - Preview Guide & What to Buy

Published March 29, 2026 | By Card Collection Club

With Perfect Order barely a week old, the Pokemon TCG machine keeps rolling. Mega Evolution: Chaos Rising drops May 22, 2026, and early reveals suggest this could be the best set of the Mega Evolution era so far. Here is everything we know and what Australian collectors should be planning.

What We Know So Far

Chaos Rising is the fourth Mega Evolution expansion, following Ascended Heroes and Perfect Order. It features over 120 cards and leans into fan-favourite Pokemon from the Kalos region.

Confirmed Headline Cards

  • Mega Greninja ex - The cover star and arguably the most popular Gen 6 starter. Expect this to be the most sought-after card in the set.
  • Mega Pyroar ex - Fire-type Mega with strong competitive potential.
  • Mega Dragalge ex - Dragon/Poison typing makes for unique art possibilities.

Why Greninja Changes Everything

Greninja is consistently one of the most popular Pokemon worldwide. It topped the Pokemon of the Year poll, dominates competitive play, and its ninja aesthetic translates perfectly to premium card art. When the Special Art Rare version drops, expect immediate demand that rivals anything we have seen from Ascended Heroes.

For context, the Mega Lucario SAR from Ascended Heroes is sitting at $120-180 AUD after just two months. Greninja has broader appeal and a bigger fanbase. Plan accordingly.

Expected Products & AU Pricing

Based on the pattern from previous Mega Evolution sets, here is what to expect:

  • Booster Box (36 packs) - Expected RRP ~$220 AUD. Pre-orders already appearing at $200-230 from AU retailers.
  • Elite Trainer Box (Greninja art) - Expected RRP ~$80 AUD. Confirmed with Greninja artwork.
  • Build & Battle Box - ~$40-50 AUD. Available at pre-release events first, then wider retail.
  • Booster Bundles - Expected ~$35-40 AUD.
  • Checklane Blisters - ~$8 AUD at Kmart, Big W, Target.

Investment Strategy for AU Collectors

Pre-Order Now

  • Lock in booster boxes at RRP from reliable AU stores. First wave allocations will be competitive given Greninja hype.
  • ETBs with Greninja art will be collectible long-term. Grab 2-3 to hold sealed.

Wait For

  • Singles market to settle (2-3 weeks post-release). Initial prices always spike then correct.
  • Second wave print runs before buying above RRP. The Pokemon Company has been good about reprints this year.

Avoid

  • Paying more than $260 AUD for a booster box pre-release. Reprints are coming.
  • Buying chase singles in the first 48 hours. The FOMO premium is real and fades fast.

Pre-Release Events in Australia

Build & Battle boxes will be available at pre-release events before the official May 22 launch. Check your local game store for dates - most Good Games, Shuffled, and independent stores will run events the weekend before release.

Pre-release is the best way to get early product at fair prices and potentially pull chase cards before the wider market opens. Plus the Build & Battle promo cards often become valuable in their own right.

The Bottom Line

Chaos Rising is shaping up to be the Greninja set everyone has been waiting for. The Mega Evolution era has been consistently strong, and having the most popular Gen 6 Pokemon as the cover star all but guarantees strong collector demand. Get your pre-orders in early, do not overpay at launch, and remember - patience beats FOMO every single time.
