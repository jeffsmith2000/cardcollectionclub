OP-15 Adventure on Kami's Island: English Week One Price Guide

April 4, 2026 · 6 min read · Card Collection Club

OP-15 Adventure on Kami's Island just hit shelves in English yesterday (April 3). The Skypiea arc set has been live in Japan since February 28, so we have a month of JP market data to guide our expectations for English pricing. Here's your week one price guide.

The Headline Cards

OP-15's chase cards are dominated by Enel and Luffy, with the Manga Rare Enel being the clear crown jewel of the set.

Enel (OP15-118) - Manga Rare [SEC-SP]

JP Market: ~$1,000+ USD

The crown jewel. Enel's Manga Rare is one of the most visually striking cards in the entire One Piece TCG. JP price stabilised around 160,000 yen ($1,000+ USD). English Manga Rares typically command a premium over JP within the first few months. Expect English copies to list at $800-$1,200 USD in week one, potentially climbing as supply tightens.

Enel (OP15-118) - Secret Rare Alternate Art [SEC-P]

JP Market: ~$58 USD (8,980 yen)

The parallel/alternate art Secret Rare. Solid card at a reasonable price point. English pricing should settle around $50-$80 USD. A good pickup for Enel fans who can't justify the Manga Rare.

Enel (OP15-118) - Secret Rare [SEC]

JP Market: ~$30-$45 USD

Regular Secret Rare Enel. JP price started at 7,000 yen and has settled around 4,500-7,000 yen. English copies should be $25-$50 USD.

Monkey D. Luffy (OP15-119) - Secret Rare Alternate Art [SEC-P]

JP Market: ~$70 USD (10,800 yen)

The alt art Luffy actually commands more than the alt art Enel in JP. Luffy cards always carry a premium. Expect $60-$100 USD for English.

Monkey D. Luffy (OP15-119) - Secret Rare [SEC]

JP Market: ~$26 USD (3,980 yen)

Regular SEC Luffy. Most accessible chase card in the set. $20-$40 USD for English.

Week One Strategy

Buy: Wait 3-5 days after English release for panic sellers to dump their pulls on TCGPlayer and eBay. Week one prices are almost always the lowest for chase cards. The Manga Rare Enel is a long-term hold if you can grab one under $900 USD.

Don't Overpay

Day 1 and Day 2 listings are often inflated by 30-50%. Let the market settle. The sweet spot for buying is typically days 4-7 when initial hype fades and more supply hits the market.

Sealed Product Pricing (AU)

  • Booster Box (24 packs): $120-$145 AUD at Australian retailers
  • Booster Pack: $7-$8 AUD
  • Expected pull rates: ~2 Secret Rares per box (based on JP data)

Where English Prices Are Heading

Based on previous set patterns, here's the likely trajectory:

  • Week 1-2: High volatility. Prices spike then dip as supply floods the market.
  • Week 3-4: Market finds a floor. This is your best buying window for singles.
  • Month 2-3: Gradual appreciation begins for top chase cards as casual openings slow down.
  • Month 6+: Manga Rares and top SEC-Ps trend upward as the set goes out of print.

Bottom Line

OP-15 is a solid set with clear chase cards. The Enel Manga Rare is the star. Don't rush to buy singles in the first 48 hours. Let the market breathe. If you're opening boxes, hold your Manga Rare pulls - they'll be worth more in 6 months than they are today.
