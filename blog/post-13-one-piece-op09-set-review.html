One Piece TCG OP-09 Set Review

By Michael H · February 18, 2026 · 8 min read

One Piece TCG continues its meteoric rise, and OP-09 might just be one of the strongest sets Bandai has released to date. Covering the Egghead Island arc — one of the most hyped storylines in the manga — this set delivers on both the competitive and collector front. Let's dive into everything you need to know.

Set Overview

OP-09 features 127 cards in the main set, plus secret rares that push the total count higher. The set focuses heavily on the Egghead Island arc, bringing us new versions of Vegapunk, Kizaru, Saturn, and the Straw Hat crew in their Egghead outfits. The set also continues support for several established archetypes while introducing new leader cards that shake up the competitive meta.

Set Details: 127+ cards | 6 Leader cards | 10 Secret Rares | Release Date: January 2026 (English)

Top Chase Cards

1. Monkey D. Luffy (Gear 5 — Manga Art Secret Rare)

The undisputed chase card of OP-09. This manga art Gear 5 Luffy features the iconic panel from the Egghead confrontation, rendered in stunning black and white with selective colour highlights. It's already one of the most sought-after cards in the entire One Piece TCG.

$350–$450 AUD

2. Kizaru (Alternate Art Secret Rare)

Admiral Kizaru's alternate art captures his light-speed attack in breathtaking detail. The composition and colour work on this card are outstanding. Competitively, this version of Kizaru is seeing play in yellow builds, which adds to its desirability.

$180–$220 AUD

3. Jewelry Bonney (Alternate Art)

Bonney has been a fan favourite throughout the Egghead arc, and her alternate art reflects that beautifully. The emotional resonance of her character combined with excellent artwork makes this a must-have for collectors.

$120–$160 AUD

4. Saint Jaygarcia Saturn (Secret Rare)

The Gorosei have finally arrived in card form, and Saturn's secret rare doesn't disappoint. The menacing artwork paired with a powerful in-game effect makes this both a collector piece and a competitive staple.

$100–$140 AUD

5. Vegapunk (Full Art Leader)

Dr. Vegapunk's leader card is one of the most versatile in the set, enabling a new blue/purple archetype that rewards careful resource management. The full art version features the scientist in his signature lab coat with Egghead Island's futuristic backdrop.

$80–$110 AUD

Competitive Impact

OP-09 has significantly shaken up the competitive landscape. Here are the key takeaways for tournament players:

  • Yellow Kizaru: A new tier 1 contender. Kizaru's leader ability provides consistent card advantage, and the supporting cast from OP-09 gives the deck real teeth.
  • Blue/Purple Vegapunk: A control-oriented deck that rewards patient play. Early results suggest it's competitive at locals and regionals.
  • Saturn Stax: A disruptive deck built around Saturn's ability to limit opponent resources. Expect to see this at higher-level events.
  • Luffy Gear 5 Upgrades: Existing Luffy builds get meaningful support cards that improve consistency.

Pull Rates

Based on our box openings and community data, here's what we're seeing:

  • Secret Rare: ~1 per 2–3 boxes (roughly 1 in 50 packs)
  • Super Rare: ~2 per box
  • Rare: ~6 per box
  • Manga Art variants: Approximately 1 in 4–5 cases for the Luffy

Pull rates feel consistent with OP-07 and OP-08, though the manga art Luffy is noticeably harder to pull than previous manga art chase cards. Bandai appears to have tightened the allocation on the top-end cards.

Box Value Analysis

At the current price of $130–$150 AUD per English booster box, OP-09 offers solid expected value. The average box yields roughly $100–$130 AUD in singles, with the potential to significantly exceed that if you hit a secret rare or the manga art Luffy. As always, opening for value is a gamble, but the floor on OP-09 boxes is higher than most recent sets.

Investment Potential

OP-09 is one of those sets we'd flag as a strong medium-term hold for sealed product. The Egghead Island arc is a defining moment in One Piece — the story revelations, the emotional beats, and the sheer spectacle make it comparable to Marineford in terms of cultural impact. Cards tied to iconic moments tend to appreciate strongly.

Our recommendation: pick up 2–3 extra booster boxes at current prices and vault them. The English print run has been more controlled than earlier sets, and demand for Egghead-era cards will only grow as the anime catches up to this arc.

Artwork Highlights

Bandai's art team has outdone themselves with OP-09. Beyond the chase cards, there are several commons and uncommons with absolutely gorgeous artwork. The Straw Hat crew in their Egghead outfits, the Seraphim designs, and the Vegapunk Satellites all feature premium illustration work that elevates the entire set.

Special mention goes to the Nico Robin alternate art, which has quietly become one of the most beloved cards in the set despite not being the highest value pull. The composition mirrors the iconic "I want to live" moment from Enies Lobby, recontextualised for Egghead.

Final Verdict

Collector Rating: ★★★★★ (5/5) — Incredible artwork, iconic moments, and genuine emotional weight.

Competitive Rating: ★★★★☆ (4/5) — Meaningful meta impact without being overpowering.

Investment Rating: ★★★★☆ (4/5) — Strong long-term potential, especially sealed product.

Overall: ★★★★★ — One of the best sets in the One Piece TCG to date.

OP-09 is a triumph. Whether you're ripping packs for the thrill, hunting chase cards for your binder, building competitive decks, or stashing sealed boxes for the future, this set delivers on every front. Bandai continues to prove that the One Piece TCG is no flash in the pan — it's a hobby mainstay.

Grab your boxes while you can, legends. This one's a keeper. 🏴‍☠️
